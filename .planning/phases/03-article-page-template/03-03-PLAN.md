**Page component:**
```typescript
export default function GuidePage({ params }: { params: { slug: string } }) {
  const guide = getGuideBySlug(params.slug);
  if (!guide) notFound();

  const category = getCategoryBySlug(guide.category);
  const readingTime = getReadingTime(guide);
  const relatedGuides = guide.relatedSlugs
    .map((slug) => getGuideBySlug(slug))
    .filter((g): g is Guide => g !== undefined)
    .map((g) => ({
      slug: g.slug,
      title: g.title,
      description: g.description,
      category: g.category,
      categoryName: getCategoryBySlug(g.category)?.name ?? g.category,
      readingTime: getReadingTime(g),
    }));

  return ( /* see layout below */ );
}
```

```mdx
<KeyTakeaways>
- You must register for VAT when turnover exceeds EUR 80,000 (goods) or EUR 40,000 (services) in any 12-month period
- Register online through Revenue's ROS system using Form TR1 (sole traders) or TR2 (companies)
- You can voluntarily register below the threshold to reclaim VAT on business purchases
- Once registered, you must file VAT returns (typically bi-monthly) and charge VAT on all taxable sales
- Late registration can result in backdated VAT liability plus interest and penalties
</KeyTakeaways>

<FAQSection>
<FAQItem question="How long does VAT registration take in Ireland?">
Revenue typically processes VAT registration applications within 3-5 working days when submitted through ROS with all required documentation. Complex applications or those requiring additional verification may take up to 2-3 weeks.
</FAQItem>
<FAQItem question="Can I register for VAT voluntarily in Ireland?">
Yes. Even if your turnover is below the mandatory thresholds, you can voluntarily register for VAT. This is beneficial if you sell primarily to VAT-registered businesses or if your input VAT (VAT on purchases) regularly exceeds your output VAT.
</FAQItem>
<FAQItem question="What happens if I don't register for VAT on time?">
If you exceed the VAT threshold and fail to register, Revenue can backdate your registration to the date you should have registered. You will owe VAT on all taxable sales from that date, plus potential interest charges and penalties.
</FAQItem>
</FAQSection>
```

```mdx
- Sole traders have simpler setup and filing but face unlimited personal liability

- Most Irish businesses start as sole traders and incorporate as they grow
- The right structure depends on your turnover, risk profile, and growth plans
</KeyTakeaways>

<FAQSection>
<FAQItem question="Can I switch from sole trader to limited company in Ireland?">
Yes. You can incorporate a limited company through the CRO at any time. You will need to register the new company for taxes with Revenue, transfer business assets, and close your sole trader tax registration. Many Irish accountants offer incorporation packages.
</FAQItem>
<FAQItem question="Do sole traders pay more tax than limited companies in Ireland?">
It depends on your profit level. Sole traders pay income tax (up to 40%), USC, and PRSI. Limited companies pay 12.5% corporation tax on profits. However, extracting profits from a company (via salary or dividends) incurs additional personal tax, so the net difference varies.
</FAQItem>
```
