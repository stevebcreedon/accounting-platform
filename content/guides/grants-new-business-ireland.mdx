Ireland offers a wide range of grants, funding, and business supports through agencies such as the Local Enterprise Offices (LEOs), Enterprise Ireland, and Microfinance Ireland. The most accessible for new small businesses are the LEO grants, which include feasibility study grants of up to EUR 15,000, priming grants of up to EUR 150,000, and the Trading Online Voucher worth up to EUR 2,500.

Navigating the Irish business support landscape can feel overwhelming. There are grants for feasibility studies, startup costs, expansion, going digital, hiring, training, and mentoring. Some are cash grants, others are repayable loans, and many come with free advisory services attached. The challenge is knowing which supports you qualify for and how to access them. This guide maps out the main grants and supports available to new businesses in Ireland, explains the eligibility criteria, and provides practical advice on how to apply successfully.

## Local Enterprise Offices (LEOs)

The Local Enterprise Offices are the first stop for most new and small businesses seeking support in Ireland. There are 31 LEOs across the country, one in each local authority area. They provide a range of financial and non-financial supports to businesses with fewer than 10 employees.

### Feasibility Study Grant

The feasibility study grant helps you research and validate your business idea before committing to a full launch. It covers costs such as market research, technical development, consultancy fees, and prototype development.

- **Maximum grant:** Up to EUR 15,000 (covers up to 50 per cent of eligible costs for sole traders; up to 60 per cent for companies)
- **Who qualifies:** Businesses at the idea or pre-startup stage that need to test whether their concept is viable
- **What it covers:** Market research, technical feasibility, consultancy, prototyping, travel for research purposes
- **Key requirement:** You must have a clear research objective and methodology. The LEO wants to see that the grant will help you make an informed go/no-go decision about the business

The feasibility study grant is competitive. You apply to your local LEO, and applications are assessed by an evaluation committee that meets regularly. A well-prepared application with a clear research plan significantly improves your chances.

### Priming Grant

The priming grant is designed for businesses in their first 18 months of trading. It helps cover the costs of establishing and growing the business.

- **Maximum grant:** Up to EUR 150,000 over the first 18 months (paid in stages)
- **Who qualifies:** New businesses with fewer than 10 employees and with the potential to grow
- **What it covers:** Capital equipment, fit-out costs, marketing, consultancy, and in some cases, employment costs
- **Key requirement:** The business must be in a sector with growth potential and must not displace existing local businesses offering similar products or services

The priming grant is the most substantial LEO grant and is highly competitive. You will need a comprehensive business plan, financial projections, and a clear growth strategy. Many successful applicants have completed a LEO Start Your Own Business programme or similar training before applying.

### Business Expansion Grant

For businesses that have been trading for more than 18 months, the business expansion grant supports further growth.

- **Maximum grant:** Up to EUR 150,000
- **Who qualifies:** Established businesses with fewer than 10 employees seeking to expand
- **What it covers:** Similar costs to the priming grant -- equipment, marketing, consultancy, employment costs
- **Key requirement:** Evidence of trading history, growth potential, and a clear expansion plan

### Technical Assistance for Micro-Enterprises (TAME)

TAME grants support specific projects such as attending trade fairs, developing marketing materials, or obtaining quality certifications.

- **Maximum grant:** Up to EUR 2,500
- **Who qualifies:** LEO client businesses with fewer than 10 employees
- **What it covers:** Trade fair attendance, website development, marketing consultancy, quality certifications
- **Application:** Less complex than the priming or feasibility grants; decided at LEO level

### Important LEO Eligibility Notes

LEO grants are available to businesses in the manufacturing and internationally traded services sectors. Retail, personal services, professional services serving the local market, and food businesses selling only domestically are generally not eligible for the larger grants. However, all businesses can access LEO training, mentoring, and the Trading Online Voucher.

The LEO assessment considers whether the business is creating employment, has export potential, and is not displacing existing local businesses. Businesses that primarily serve the domestic market or are in sectors with existing local competition may be directed towards other supports instead.

## Enterprise Ireland

Enterprise Ireland supports Irish-owned businesses with the ambition and capability to achieve significant scale through exports. Their supports are aimed at businesses with higher growth potential than the typical LEO client.

### Competitive Start Fund (CSF)

The CSF provides equity investment to early-stage startups with the potential to create employment and generate export sales.

- **Investment:** Up to EUR 50,000 in equity (Enterprise Ireland takes a small equity stake in your company)
- **Who qualifies:** Early-stage companies with an innovative product or service and clear export potential
- **What it covers:** The investment can be used for product development, market validation, and early commercialisation
- **Key requirement:** The business must be incorporated as a limited company, have a scalable product or service, and be targeting export markets

The CSF is highly competitive and runs in cycles with application deadlines. Successful applicants typically have a strong founding team, a differentiated product, and a credible path to international sales.

### High Potential Start-Up (HPSU) Funding

For businesses with the potential to achieve EUR 1 million or more in export sales and create 10 or more jobs within 3 years, Enterprise Ireland offers HPSU funding.

- **Investment:** Equity investments typically ranging from EUR 50,000 to EUR 500,000
- **Who qualifies:** Companies with a scalable, innovative product or service targeting international markets
- **What it covers:** Product development, recruitment, market entry, and scaling
- **Key requirement:** A detailed business plan, strong management team, significant market opportunity, and willingness to accept Enterprise Ireland equity investment

HPSU funding comes with significant support beyond the financial investment, including a dedicated development adviser, access to Enterprise Ireland's international office network, and introductions to potential customers and partners.

### Innovation Vouchers

Innovation vouchers are worth EUR 5,000 each and allow small businesses to work with a registered knowledge provider (university, institute of technology, or public research body) on a specific innovation project.

- **Value:** EUR 5,000 per voucher (businesses can apply for up to 3 vouchers worth a total of EUR 15,000)
- **Who qualifies:** Irish-registered small businesses with fewer than 50 employees
- **What it covers:** Research, testing, product development, technical advice from a registered knowledge provider
- **Application:** Online through Enterprise Ireland's website

Innovation vouchers are accessible and relatively straightforward to obtain. They are ideal for businesses that need expert technical input but cannot afford to commission a full research project.

## Microfinance Ireland

Microfinance Ireland provides loans to small businesses that have been declined by a bank or that are unlikely to qualify for traditional bank lending. It is a government-backed initiative designed to fill the gap between grant funding and commercial lending.

- **Loan amounts:** EUR 2,000 to EUR 25,000 (up to EUR 50,000 for a second loan)
- **Interest rate:** Competitive fixed rates, typically below commercial bank rates
- **Repayment terms:** Up to 5 years
- **Who qualifies:** Sole traders, partnerships, and limited companies with fewer than 10 employees and turnover under EUR 2 million
- **Key requirement:** You must have been declined by a bank or demonstrate that bank lending is not available to you. Apply through your local LEO, which refers your application to Microfinance Ireland

The application process involves completing a loan application form and submitting a business plan and financial projections. Your local LEO can help you prepare your application and provide a referral.

## Back to Work Enterprise Allowance (BTWEA)

The BTWEA is a Department of Social Protection scheme that allows people on certain social welfare payments to keep a percentage of their payment for up to 2 years while starting a business.

- **Year 1:** You retain 100 per cent of your social welfare payment
- **Year 2:** You retain 75 per cent of your social welfare payment
- **Who qualifies:** People who have been receiving Jobseeker's Allowance for at least 9 months (or 12 months for some variants), or certain other qualifying payments such as Disability Allowance or One-Parent Family Payment
- **Key requirement:** You must have a viable business plan approved by a Partnership Company (local development company) or your local DSP case officer

The BTWEA provides a financial safety net while you build your business. It is particularly valuable because it allows you to retain your secondary benefits (medical card, rent supplement) during the first year.

To apply, contact your local Intreo Centre or Social Welfare Branch Office. You will need to present a business plan and demonstrate that the business is viable.

## Short-Term Enterprise Allowance (STEA)

The STEA is similar to the BTWEA but is designed for people who have recently become unemployed and want to start a business immediately, without the 9-month waiting period.

- **Duration:** Up to 9 months
- **Payment:** You receive your Jobseeker's Benefit entitlement in full
- **Who qualifies:** People currently receiving Jobseeker's Benefit (the contributory payment based on PRSI contributions) who want to become self-employed
- **Key requirement:** A viable business plan

The STEA allows you to use the remainder of your Jobseeker's Benefit entitlement to support yourself while starting the business. Once the STEA period ends, you may be able to transfer to the BTWEA for further support.

## Trading Online Voucher (TOV)

The Trading Online Voucher is one of the most popular LEO supports. It helps small businesses develop or improve their online trading presence.

- **Value:** Up to EUR 2,500 (the LEO pays 50 per cent of eligible costs, so a EUR 5,000 project costs you EUR 2,500)
- **Who qualifies:** Businesses with fewer than 10 employees and turnover under EUR 2 million that have limited or no online trading presence
- **What it covers:** Website development, e-commerce platforms, online booking systems, digital marketing setup, training related to online trading
- **Key requirement:** You must attend a LEO seminar on trading online before applying, and the project must result in a measurable improvement in your online trading capability

The application process involves completing a form through your local LEO, including a brief plan for how you will use the voucher. Applications are assessed regularly, and successful applicants must complete their project within 6 months.

A second TOV of up to EUR 2,500 may be available if you have fully utilised your first voucher and can demonstrate further online development needs.

## Mentoring Programmes

Mentoring is one of the most underutilised supports available to Irish businesses. Several agencies offer free or subsidised mentoring to new business owners.

### LEO Mentoring

Every LEO offers a panel of experienced business mentors. As a LEO client, you can request mentoring sessions (typically 3 to 6 sessions) at no cost. Mentors are experienced business owners, managers, or specialists who can advise on business planning, marketing, finance, operations, and growth strategy.

### Enterprise Ireland Mentor Network

Enterprise Ireland maintains a network of mentors for its client companies. These mentors are typically experienced entrepreneurs or executives with relevant industry expertise. Mentoring is provided as part of Enterprise Ireland's broader support package for client companies.

### Volunteer Mentoring Programmes

Organisations such as Mentor Works and various industry-specific networks offer volunteer mentoring programmes. These can be valuable for getting sector-specific advice from people who have built businesses in your industry.

## How to Apply Successfully

Having worked with the LEO and Enterprise Ireland systems, here are practical tips for a successful grant application:

**Start with your LEO.** Even if you think your business is more suited to Enterprise Ireland, start by engaging with your local LEO. They can assess your eligibility across all programmes and direct you to the most appropriate support. Many Enterprise Ireland clients were originally LEO clients who graduated as their business grew.

**Attend the training first.** LEOs offer free Start Your Own Business programmes, trading online seminars, and various workshops. Completing these before applying for a grant demonstrates commitment and also helps you refine your business plan.

**Prepare a solid business plan.** Every grant application requires a business plan. This should include market analysis, financial projections (3 years minimum), marketing strategy, and an operations plan. If you are unsure how to write one, your LEO offers training and mentoring to help.

**Be specific about how you will use the funding.** Grant evaluators want to see a clear, itemised breakdown of what the grant will fund and how each item contributes to business growth. Vague requests for "general business development" will not succeed.

**Show job creation potential.** Most state grants are assessed partly on their employment creation potential. Even if you are a sole trader now, demonstrating a realistic plan to hire employees strengthens your application.

**Apply early in your journey.** Do not wait until you are in financial difficulty to seek grant support. The best time to apply is when you have a clear plan and can use the funding proactively for growth, not reactively for survival.

**Keep records of everything.** Grant funding typically requires you to provide receipts and evidence of expenditure. Maintain meticulous records from day one.
