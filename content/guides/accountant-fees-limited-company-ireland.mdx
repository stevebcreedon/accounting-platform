A limited company in Ireland typically pays between EUR 1,500 and EUR 3,500 per year for accounting services. This is substantially more than sole trader fees because limited companies have greater compliance obligations, including statutory financial statements, corporation tax returns, CRO annual filings, and company secretarial requirements. This guide explains exactly what you should expect to pay, what is included at each price level, and how to structure your accounting engagement to get the best value.

The gap between the lowest and highest fees in this range is significant -- EUR 2,000 per year -- and understanding what drives that difference allows you to make informed decisions when choosing or renegotiating with your accountant.

## Core Compliance Package: What Is Always Included

Every limited company in Ireland must complete certain statutory obligations each year. The core compliance package that your accountant provides covers these obligations and forms the baseline of your annual fee.

### Annual Financial Statements

Your accountant prepares statutory financial statements in accordance with FRS 102 (or FRS 102 Section 1A for small companies). These include:

- **Profit and loss account** (income statement) showing your revenue, costs, and net profit for the financial year
- **Balance sheet** (statement of financial position) showing your assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity at the year end
- **Notes to the accounts** disclosing accounting policies, related party transactions, directors' remuneration, and other required information
- **Directors' report** summarising the company's activities, results, and dividends

For small companies qualifying for the audit exemption, abridged accounts can be filed with the CRO. Your accountant prepares both the full accounts (for Revenue and internal use) and the abridged version (for public filing).

### Corporation Tax Return (CT1)

The Form CT1 is the annual corporation tax return filed with Revenue. Your accountant calculates your corporation tax liability at 12.5 per cent on trading income (or 25 per cent on passive/investment income) and completes the detailed return covering:

- Trading income and allowable deductions
- Capital allowances on plant, machinery, and equipment
- Close company surcharge calculations (applicable to most small companies)
- Preliminary tax calculations and payment scheduling
- Any reliefs claimed (R&D credit, start-up relief, loss relief)

### CRO Annual Return (B1 Form)

Every company must file an annual return with the Companies Registration Office within 28 days of its annual return date. Your accountant prepares and files the B1 form along with the abridged financial statements. The CRO filing fee of EUR 20 is usually passed through to you separately.

Late filing penalties are severe -- EUR 100 per day up to a maximum of EUR 5,000 -- and a late filing causes the loss of audit exemption for the current and following financial year. Your accountant's management of this deadline is a critical part of the service.

### Director's Personal Tax Return

Most limited company accounting packages include at least one director's personal Form 11 income tax return. This is essential because company directors are chargeable persons under the self-assessment system and must file a personal return even if all their income comes through the company payroll.

The Form 11 captures the director's salary, dividends, benefit-in-kind (company car, health insurance), rental income, and any other personal income. The close relationship between the company's and director's tax affairs means it makes sense for the same accountant to handle both.

## Fee Breakdown by Company Size

### Micro Company: EUR 1,500 to EUR 2,000

A micro company in this context means:

- One or two directors, no other employees
- Turnover under EUR 500,000
- Simple trading activity with straightforward revenue and expenses
- No VAT registration or simple VAT affairs
- Well-maintained bookkeeping records

At this level, the accountant's work is largely compliance-focused. The annual accounts and CT1 preparation are straightforward, and the director's Form 11 has limited complexity.

### Small Company: EUR 2,000 to EUR 2,800

A small company typically has:

- Two to five employees on payroll
- Turnover between EUR 500,000 and EUR 2 million
- VAT-registered with regular bi-monthly returns
- Some complexity in the accounts (debtors, creditors, accruals, stock)
- Multiple directors with different remuneration structures

The additional complexity comes from payroll obligations, VAT compliance, and more detailed financial statements. The CT1 return is more involved due to higher transaction volumes and potentially more complex tax computations.

### Growing Company: EUR 2,800 to EUR 3,500+

A growing company at the upper end of the fee range typically has:

- Five to twenty employees
- Turnover exceeding EUR 2 million
- Complex VAT affairs (multiple rates, EU trade, partial exemption)
- Management accounts requirements
- Inter-company transactions or group structures
- Capital allowance claims on significant asset purchases
- R&D tax credit claims

At this level, the accountant provides advisory services alongside compliance work, including tax planning around director remuneration, dividend policy, and business structuring.

## Additional Services Charged Separately

Many limited company accounting packages include only the core compliance work. The following services are commonly charged as extras.

### Payroll Processing

Payroll is one of the most significant additional costs for limited companies with employees. Typical pricing:

- **EUR 5 to EUR 10 per employee per pay run** for monthly payroll
- **EUR 5 to EUR 15 per employee per pay run** for weekly payroll
- **Payroll setup for a new employee:** EUR 30 to EUR 75 (Revenue registration, tax credit application)
- **Year-end payroll reconciliation:** Often included in the annual fee, but some firms charge EUR 100 to EUR 200

For a company with five employees on monthly payroll, the annual payroll cost is approximately EUR 300 to EUR 600. For ten employees on weekly payroll, payroll costs can reach EUR 2,600 to EUR 7,800 per year -- sometimes exceeding the core accounting fee.

Your accountant handles PAYE, PRSI, USC, and Local Property Tax deductions through Revenue's enhanced reporting system. Each pay run requires submission to Revenue within the statutory timeframe.

### VAT Returns

Bi-monthly VAT3 returns are a significant additional cost:

- **EUR 80 to EUR 150 per VAT3 return** from organised bookkeeping records
- **EUR 150 to EUR 250 per return** if the accountant prepares from source documents
- **Annual RTD (Return of Trading Details):** EUR 80 to EUR 150
- **Total annual VAT cost:** EUR 560 to EUR 1,050

Companies with EU trade (Intrastat filings, VIES returns) or reverse charge transactions face higher costs due to the additional complexity.

### Management Accounts

Monthly or quarterly management accounts provide internal financial reporting for business decision-making:

- **Quarterly management accounts:** EUR 200 to EUR 400 per quarter (EUR 800 to EUR 1,600 per year)
- **Monthly management accounts:** EUR 150 to EUR 300 per month (EUR 1,800 to EUR 3,600 per year)

Management accounts typically include a profit and loss statement, balance sheet, cash flow summary, and variance analysis against budget or prior year. They are not a legal requirement but are valuable for any company with turnover above EUR 500,000 or those with external investors or lenders who require regular reporting.

### Company Secretarial Services

Limited companies have ongoing company secretarial obligations under the Companies Act 2014:

- Maintaining statutory registers (members, directors, secretary, charges)
- Filing notifications of changes with the CRO (director appointments, registered office changes, share transfers)
- Preparing and distributing AGM minutes
- Managing share certificates and transfers
- Annual compliance calendar management

Typical cost: EUR 200 to EUR 500 per year, depending on the volume of changes. Some accountants include basic secretarial work in the annual fee; others charge separately for each filing.

### Bookkeeping

If your accountant provides regular bookkeeping services rather than just year-end accounts preparation:

- **Monthly bookkeeping (small company, under 100 transactions/month):** EUR 200 to EUR 400 per month
- **Monthly bookkeeping (medium company, 100-500 transactions/month):** EUR 400 to EUR 800 per month
- **Bank reconciliation only:** EUR 50 to EUR 150 per month

Many companies handle day-to-day bookkeeping in-house using Xero, Sage, or QuickBooks and engage their accountant only for year-end adjustments and compliance work. This is the most cost-effective approach if you have someone competent to manage the software.

## Retainer vs Pay-Per-Service

Limited companies can structure their accounting engagement in two main ways.

### Annual Retainer (Most Common)

A fixed annual fee covers a defined scope of services, typically paid monthly or quarterly. The retainer provides fee certainty and encourages you to use the advisory component throughout the year rather than saving all your questions for the year-end meeting.

Advantages:
- Predictable monthly cost for budgeting
- Encourages proactive communication with your accountant
- Accountant is invested in understanding your business year-round
- Typically better value than paying per service

### Pay-Per-Service

Individual services priced and invoiced separately. This suits companies with very simple affairs that need minimal accountant interaction beyond the annual compliance work.

Advantages:
- Only pay for what you use
- Flexibility to use different providers for different services
- No minimum commitment

Disadvantages:
- Total cost can exceed a retainer if you need frequent input
- Creates a financial barrier to seeking advice
- Accountant may be less invested in your business

For most limited companies, a retainer arrangement with clearly defined scope provides the best balance of value and certainty. Ensure the engagement letter specifies exactly what is included and what triggers additional charges.

## Negotiating Your Accountant Fees

Limited company accounting fees are negotiable, particularly when you are a new client or when market conditions are competitive. Effective negotiation strategies include:

**Bundle services.** If you need accounts preparation, CT1, payroll, VAT returns, and company secretarial work, negotiate a bundled price rather than accepting individual service quotes. Accountants prefer bundled engagements because they provide predictable revenue and reduce client churn.

**Commit to good record keeping.** Offer to maintain your bookkeeping in cloud software (preferably the accountant's preferred platform) and provide clean, reconciled data. This reduces the accountant's workload and justifies a lower fee.

**Multi-year commitment.** Some accountants offer a fee discount for a two or three-year engagement, particularly for newer companies that represent long-term growth potential.

**Refer other businesses.** Many accountants offer referral discounts or credits for introducing new clients. If you know other business owners looking for accounting services, this can meaningfully reduce your costs.

**Review annually.** Do not let your fee increase automatically year after year without questioning it. If your business has not changed materially, your fee should not increase beyond inflation. If it has changed, ensure the increase is proportionate to the additional work involved.

## Economies of Scale

As your company grows, the per-unit cost of accounting services generally decreases. A company with EUR 2 million turnover does not pay twice as much as a company with EUR 1 million turnover, because much of the compliance work (CT1, B1, financial statements) has a fixed time component regardless of size.

However, growth also introduces new complexities -- more employees (payroll costs), more transactions (bookkeeping volume), potentially crossing audit thresholds, EU trade, and R&D activities. Each new complexity adds incremental cost.

The most cost-effective growth strategy from an accounting perspective is to invest in internal bookkeeping capability (a good bookkeeper or office manager using cloud software) so that your accountant focuses on compliance, tax planning, and advisory work rather than data processing.

## Audit vs Non-Audit Companies

Most small Irish companies qualify for audit exemption under the Companies Act 2014. To qualify, a company must meet two of the following three thresholds:

- Turnover not exceeding EUR 12 million
- Balance sheet total not exceeding EUR 6 million
- Average number of employees not exceeding 50

If your company qualifies for the audit exemption, you do not need a statutory audit, and your accounting fees reflect only the accounts preparation and compliance work described above.

If your company exceeds these thresholds (or is required to have an audit for other reasons, such as being part of a group), audit fees are a substantial additional cost -- typically EUR 3,000 to EUR 15,000 depending on company size and complexity. This is separate from your ongoing accounting and tax compliance fees.
