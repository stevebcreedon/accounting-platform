A personal income tax return (Form 11) in Ireland typically costs between EUR 200 and EUR 500 when prepared by an accountant. A corporation tax return (CT1) for a limited company costs EUR 500 to EUR 1,500. The exact price depends on the complexity of your income, the quality of your records, and whether the return is filed as a standalone service or as part of a broader accounting package. This guide breaks down the cost of every type of tax return filed in Ireland and explains what affects the price.

Understanding what you are paying for -- and why one accountant charges EUR 250 while another charges EUR 500 for the same type of return -- allows you to make informed decisions and avoid overpaying.

## Form 11 Income Tax Return

The Form 11 is the annual self-assessment income tax return for individuals in Ireland. It is required for all self-employed persons, company directors, individuals with non-PAYE income exceeding EUR 5,000, and anyone with certain investment or rental income.

### What It Costs

| Complexity Level | Typical Cost | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Simple | EUR 200-300 | Single trade income, few expenses, no rental or investment income |
| Standard | EUR 300-450 | Trade income plus rental income or multiple income sources |
| Complex | EUR 450-600+ | Multiple income sources, capital gains, foreign income, complex reliefs |

### What Is Included

When your accountant prepares your Form 11, the work typically includes:

- Reviewing your income records and supporting documentation
- Preparing your trading profit and loss account (if self-employed)
- Calculating capital allowances on business assets
- Identifying all allowable deductions and reliefs (personal tax credits, medical expenses, pension contributions, home carer credit, rent credit)
- Completing all relevant sections of the Form 11 (there are over 30 panels covering different income types)
- Calculating your income tax, USC, and PRSI liabilities
- Calculating your preliminary tax obligation for the current year
- Filing the return via ROS before the deadline
- Providing you with a summary of your liability and payment dates

### What Affects the Price

**Number of income sources.** A sole trader with only trading income has a straightforward Form 11. Add rental income from two properties, some dividend income, and a capital gain from a share disposal, and the return becomes significantly more complex. Each additional income source requires its own calculations, reliefs, and disclosures.

**Quality of records.** If you provide clean, organised records (a profit and loss account, bank statements, a summary of rental income and expenses), your accountant can work efficiently. If they need to reconstruct your records from raw bank statements and receipts, the additional time adds EUR 100 to EUR 300 to the cost.

**Capital gains.** Disposing of assets (property, shares, business assets) during the year requires capital gains tax calculations on the Form 11. Each disposal involves calculating the cost base, indexation relief (for pre-2003 acquisitions), and the applicable CGT rate (33 per cent). Complex disposals (part-disposals, connected party transactions, entrepreneur relief claims) add significant work.

**Foreign income.** Income from overseas sources requires double taxation relief calculations. You must declare the gross foreign income, claim credit for foreign tax paid, and navigate the relevant double taxation agreement. This adds EUR 100 to EUR 200 to the cost of your return.

**Lateness.** If your return is filed late or if you bring your records to your accountant close to the deadline, many firms apply a surcharge. The rush work disrupts their schedule and carries the risk of errors. Some accountants charge 20 to 50 per cent more for returns completed within the final two weeks before the filing deadline.

## CT1 Corporation Tax Return

The Form CT1 is the annual corporation tax return for Irish limited companies. It is more complex than a personal Form 11 and requires supporting financial statements.

### What It Costs

| Company Type | Typical Cost | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Micro (1-2 directors, simple trade) | EUR 500-800 | Straightforward trading company, few transactions |
| Small (under EUR 1m turnover) | EUR 800-1,200 | Standard small company, moderate complexity |
| Medium (EUR 1m-5m turnover) | EUR 1,200-1,500+ | Higher complexity, multiple tax rates, R&D credits |

These costs are for the CT1 preparation and filing only. Most companies have their CT1 prepared as part of an annual accounting package that also includes financial statements and the CRO annual return. The standalone CT1 cost is relevant if you prepare your own accounts and only need the tax return filed.

### What Is Included

The CT1 preparation involves:

- Reviewing the financial statements and adjusting accounting profit for tax purposes
- Calculating trading income with add-backs for disallowable expenses
- Applying the correct corporation tax rate (12.5 per cent on trading income, 25 per cent on passive income)
- Calculating capital allowances on plant, machinery, vehicles, and other qualifying assets
- Applying loss relief (current year, carry forward, or carry back)
- Calculating the close company surcharge on undistributed investment and estate income
- Completing the detailed CT1 return (income, deductions, reliefs, credits, payments)
- Calculating preliminary tax and payment obligations
- Filing via ROS

### What Affects the Price

**Company complexity.** A single-trade company with one bank account and a handful of transactions is fast to process. A company with multiple revenue streams, intercompany transactions, stock valuations, and foreign currency dealings takes substantially longer.

**Close company surcharge.** Most small Irish companies are close companies (controlled by five or fewer shareholders). The surcharge calculation on undistributed investment income adds complexity, particularly when the company has rental income, interest income, or other passive income alongside its trade.

**R&D tax credit claims.** The R&D tax credit (25 per cent of qualifying expenditure) is valuable but requires detailed documentation of qualifying activities and expenditure. Preparing an R&D credit claim typically adds EUR 500 to EUR 2,000 to the cost, depending on the scale of the claim.

**Start-up relief.** New companies may qualify for relief from corporation tax in their first three years of trading (under Section 486C TCA). Claiming this relief requires specific calculations and conditions to be met, adding modest complexity to the return.

## VAT Return Costs

VAT-registered businesses in Ireland must file bi-monthly VAT3 returns (six per year) plus an annual Return of Trading Details (RTD).

### What It Costs

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Single VAT3 return (from bookkeeping records) | EUR 50-100 |
| Single VAT3 return (from source documents) | EUR 100-150 |
| Annual RTD | EUR 50-100 |
| Annual total (6 x VAT3 + RTD) | EUR 350-700 |

### What Affects the Price

**Record quality.** If your bookkeeping software produces a VAT-ready report showing output VAT (sales) and input VAT (purchases) broken down by rate, your accountant's work is primarily review and filing. If they must calculate VAT from invoices and bank statements, each return takes significantly longer.

**Complexity of VAT affairs.** Businesses dealing with multiple VAT rates (23%, 13.5%, 9%, 0%, exempt), EU acquisitions, reverse charge services, or partial exemption calculations face higher costs per return. Cross-border trade involving Intrastat or VIES returns adds further complexity.

**Transaction volume.** A business with 20 sales invoices per month is quicker to process than one with 200. High-volume businesses benefit from robust bookkeeping software that automates VAT calculations.

## CGT Returns

Capital gains tax in Ireland is charged at 33 per cent on gains from the disposal of assets. CGT is reported on the Form 11 (for individuals) or CT1 (for companies), but it also has its own interim filing obligations.

### What It Costs

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Simple CGT calculation (single disposal) | EUR 100-250 |
| Complex CGT (multiple disposals, entrepreneur relief) | EUR 250-500+ |
| CGT return filing (Form CG1 or Form 11 section) | Included in above |

### What Affects the Price

- **Number of disposals:** Each asset disposal requires a separate calculation
- **Type of asset:** Property disposals involve conveyancing documents, enhancement expenditure, and potentially principal private residence relief. Share disposals require cost base calculations, possibly across multiple purchase dates
- **Reliefs claimed:** Entrepreneur relief (10 per cent rate on qualifying gains up to EUR 1 million lifetime), retirement relief, and revised entrepreneur relief all require specific conditions to be verified and documented

## PAYE Returns and Employer Obligations

Employers in Ireland submit payroll data to Revenue in real time through the enhanced reporting system. While this is an ongoing obligation rather than an annual return, there are costs associated with payroll compliance.

### What It Costs

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Per-employee per pay run | EUR 5-15 |
| Payroll setup per new employee | EUR 30-75 |
| Year-end payroll reconciliation | EUR 100-200 |
| P35 / employer annual summary | Often included in annual fee |

Payroll costs are typically charged as part of an ongoing accounting engagement rather than as a standalone tax return service. See the section on payroll costs for a more detailed breakdown.

## DIY vs Professional Tax Return Filing

You are not legally required to use an accountant to file your tax return in Ireland. Revenue's ROS system is accessible to all registered taxpayers, and Revenue provides comprehensive help guides.

### When DIY Makes Sense

Filing your own return can be cost-effective if:

- You have a single income source (PAYE employment or simple sole trade)
- You are comfortable using ROS and navigating the Form 11 panels
- Your affairs are straightforward with no rental income, capital gains, or foreign income
- You are confident identifying all allowable deductions and credits

### When a Professional Makes Sense

Using an accountant is usually worth the cost when:

- You have multiple income sources requiring coordination
- You are self-employed and need trading accounts prepared
- You have rental properties with mortgage interest, capital allowances, and expenses to calculate
- You have disposed of assets and need CGT calculations
- You are a company director with benefit-in-kind considerations
- You want peace of mind that your return is correct and complete
- You want to minimise your tax liability through legitimate planning

### The Cost of Errors

Filing an incorrect return carries financial consequences. Revenue can charge:

- **Surcharge for late filing:** 5 per cent of tax due (up to EUR 12,695) if filed within 2 months of the deadline, or 10 per cent (up to EUR 63,485) if later
- **Interest on late payment:** Approximately 8 per cent per annum (0.0219 per cent per day)
- **Penalties for incorrect returns:** Tax-geared penalties ranging from 3 per cent to 100 per cent of the underpaid tax, depending on whether the error was careless, deliberate, or deliberate with concealment

A single error that triggers a Revenue compliance check can cost far more in time, stress, and penalties than years of professional accounting fees.

## Using ROS Yourself

If you decide to file your own return, Revenue Online Service (ROS) is the platform you will use. ROS is free to access and available to all registered taxpayers.

### Getting Started with ROS

To register for ROS, you need:

1. Your PPS number
2. Your tax registration number (if different)
3. A ROS digital certificate (requested online, access code sent by post)

Once registered, you can:

- Complete and file your Form 11 online
- View your tax position and payment history
- Make tax payments via debit card or bank transfer
- Correspond with Revenue electronically
- Access Revenue's online calculators for income tax, CGT, and CAT

### Limitations of DIY Filing

While ROS is user-friendly, it does not provide:

- Guidance on what expenses are deductible for your specific situation
- Calculations of complex reliefs (entrepreneur relief, artist's exemption, start-up relief)
- Tax planning advice on optimal salary/dividend splits for directors
- Review of your records for completeness or accuracy
- Representation if Revenue selects you for a compliance check

ROS is a filing tool, not an advisory service. It will accept whatever figures you enter without questioning whether they are correct or optimal.

## Penalties for Tax Return Errors

Understanding the penalty regime helps you assess the true cost/benefit of professional tax return preparation versus DIY filing.

Revenue applies a penalty classification system based on the taxpayer's behaviour:

| Behaviour | Penalty Range | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Innocent error | No penalty | Genuine mistake despite reasonable care |
| Insufficient care | 3%-15% of underpaid tax | Carelessness, not deliberate |
| Deliberate but not concealed | 20%-75% of underpaid tax | Intentional understatement |
| Deliberate and concealed | 50%-100% of underpaid tax | Fraud or wilful evasion |

Penalties are reduced if the taxpayer makes an unprompted qualifying disclosure before Revenue initiates an audit. The difference between prompted and unprompted disclosure can halve the penalty amount.

For most business owners, the risk of "insufficient care" penalties is the primary concern. Using a qualified accountant is strong evidence of reasonable care in your tax affairs, which can be decisive if Revenue questions your return.

## How to Get the Best Value

To minimise what you pay for tax return preparation while ensuring accuracy:

1. **Start early.** Give your accountant your records at least two months before the filing deadline. Last-minute submissions attract higher fees.
2. **Organise your records.** Sort income by source, expenses by category, and provide bank statements in digital format. A clear summary of the year's financial activity saves your accountant hours.
3. **Use bookkeeping software.** Even a basic setup in Xero or Sage produces reports that dramatically reduce your accountant's data processing time.
4. **Ask about package pricing.** If you need multiple services (Form 11, rental income calculations, CGT), a package price is usually cheaper than paying for each separately.
5. **Communicate changes proactively.** If you started a new business, bought a rental property, or sold shares during the year, tell your accountant early so they can plan the work and quote accurately.
