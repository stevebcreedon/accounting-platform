A bookkeeper in Ireland typically charges EUR 25 to EUR 50 per hour, or EUR 100 to EUR 500 per month for a regular bookkeeping package. The exact cost depends on the volume of transactions your business processes, the complexity of your accounts, whether you need VAT return preparation, and whether you engage an in-house employee, an outsourced bookkeeping service, or a virtual bookkeeper. This guide covers all the pricing models, what is included at each level, and how to decide which approach suits your business.

Getting your bookkeeping right is one of the most cost-effective investments a small business can make. Poor bookkeeping inflates your accountant's year-end fees, increases the risk of tax errors, and makes it harder to understand your business's financial health.

## Hourly Rates for Bookkeepers in Ireland

Bookkeeping hourly rates vary based on the bookkeeper's qualifications, experience, and location.

### Typical Hourly Rates

| Bookkeeper Level | Hourly Rate | Typical Qualifications |
|---|---|---|
| Junior/trainee bookkeeper | EUR 18-25 | Studying for Accounting Technicians Ireland (ATI) qualification |
| Experienced bookkeeper | EUR 25-40 | ATI qualified, several years of experience |
| Senior bookkeeper / part-qualified accountant | EUR 35-50 | ATI qualified plus additional experience or part ACCA/CPA |
| Specialist (payroll, VAT, multi-currency) | EUR 40-55 | Specialist certifications, extensive experience |

### What Affects the Hourly Rate

**Qualifications.** Bookkeepers qualified through Accounting Technicians Ireland (ATI) command higher rates than those without formal qualifications. The ATI qualification demonstrates competence in double-entry bookkeeping, computerised accounts, payroll, and tax fundamentals.

**Experience.** A bookkeeper with ten years of experience across multiple industries is more efficient and makes fewer errors than one with two years of experience. The higher hourly rate is often offset by the lower number of hours needed to complete the same work.

**Location.** Dublin-based bookkeepers charge 10 to 20 per cent more than those outside Dublin, reflecting higher living costs and market rates. However, remote and virtual bookkeeping has become standard, so you are not limited to bookkeepers in your geographic area.

**Software proficiency.** Bookkeepers who are certified in specific software platforms (Xero Certified Advisor, Sage Accounts, QuickBooks ProAdvisor) can charge a premium because they work more efficiently within these systems and can handle setup, troubleshooting, and reporting.

## Monthly Bookkeeping Packages

Most bookkeeping services in Ireland offer monthly packages based on transaction volume rather than hourly billing. This provides cost certainty and aligns the bookkeeper's effort with your business's actual workload.

### Package Pricing

| Monthly Volume | Monthly Cost | What Is Included |
|---|---|---|
| Under 50 transactions | EUR 100-200 | Data entry, bank reconciliation, basic reporting |
| 50-150 transactions | EUR 200-350 | Data entry, bank reconciliation, supplier payments, VAT preparation |
| 150-300 transactions | EUR 350-500 | Full bookkeeping, bank reconciliation, VAT returns, invoicing, debtor management |
| 300+ transactions | EUR 500-800+ | All of the above plus payroll preparation, management reports, multi-currency |

A "transaction" in this context typically means a single entry in your accounting records -- one sales invoice, one purchase invoice, one bank receipt, or one payment. Some bookkeepers count bank lines instead of source documents, which produces a different transaction count for the same business.

### What Is Included in a Typical Monthly Package

A standard monthly bookkeeping package covers the ongoing financial record-keeping that your business needs:

**Data entry.** Recording all sales invoices, purchase invoices, receipts, and payments in your accounting software. This is the core of bookkeeping and the most time-consuming element for high-volume businesses.

**Bank reconciliation.** Matching every transaction in your bank account to a corresponding entry in your accounting records, and investigating any discrepancies. This should be done at least monthly to catch errors, fraud, or missing transactions early.

**Accounts receivable (debtors).** Raising sales invoices, tracking payments, sending statements and payment reminders, and managing overdue debts. Some bookkeepers include basic credit control in their package; others charge it as an extra.

**Accounts payable (creditors).** Recording supplier invoices, scheduling payments, and maintaining the purchase ledger. Some packages include preparing payment runs (bank transfer files) for your approval.

**VAT preparation.** Calculating your VAT liability from the bookkeeping records and preparing the VAT3 return for your accountant (or for your own filing). Note that your accountant typically reviews and files the return, even if the bookkeeper prepares it.

**Monthly reporting.** Producing a basic profit and loss statement and balance sheet at month end. More detailed management reports (cash flow, budget variance, aged debtors) are usually available at the upper package levels.

## In-House vs Outsourced Bookkeeping

The decision between hiring an in-house bookkeeper and outsourcing to a bookkeeping service significantly affects your total cost.

### In-House Employee Bookkeeper

Hiring a part-time or full-time bookkeeper as an employee provides dedicated support but carries substantial additional costs beyond the salary.

**Part-time bookkeeper (20 hours per week):**

| Cost Component | Annual Amount |
|---|---|
| Gross salary (EUR 15-20/hour) | EUR 15,600-20,800 |
| Employer PRSI (11.05%) | EUR 1,724-2,298 |
| Annual leave (pro-rata) | Included in salary cost |
| Sick pay provision | EUR 500-1,000 |
| Software licence | EUR 200-500 |
| Training / CPD | EUR 200-500 |
| **Total annual cost** | **EUR 18,224-25,098** |

**Full-time bookkeeper (39 hours per week):**

| Cost Component | Annual Amount |
|---|---|
| Gross salary (EUR 28,000-38,000) | EUR 28,000-38,000 |
| Employer PRSI (11.05%) | EUR 3,094-4,199 |
| Annual leave (20 days minimum) | Included in salary cost |
| Sick pay provision | EUR 800-1,500 |
| Software licence | EUR 200-500 |
| Training / CPD | EUR 300-600 |
| Pension contribution (auto-enrolment coming) | EUR 840-1,140 |
| **Total annual cost** | **EUR 33,234-45,939** |

The employer PRSI rate of 11.05 per cent adds a significant cost on top of the gross salary. This is a mandatory employer contribution that applies to all employees earning above the weekly PRSI threshold.

Pension auto-enrolment is scheduled for introduction in Ireland, requiring employers to contribute to employee pensions. While the exact start date and contribution rates are being finalised, budgeting for this additional cost is prudent.

### Outsourced Bookkeeping Service

Outsourcing your bookkeeping to a specialist service or freelance bookkeeper provides flexibility and typically costs less than an in-house employee for small to medium businesses.

**Advantages:**
- Pay only for hours or services used -- no PRSI, pension, sick pay, or annual leave costs
- Access to experienced, qualified bookkeepers without recruitment
- Continuity of service (the service handles cover for illness and holidays)
- Software and training costs are the service's responsibility
- Scalable -- increase or decrease hours as your business demands change

**Disadvantages:**
- Less immediate availability than an on-site employee
- The bookkeeper works with multiple clients, so response times may vary
- Less deep knowledge of your specific business operations
- Data security considerations when sharing financial information externally

**Cost comparison:** A business with 100 transactions per month might pay EUR 250 per month (EUR 3,000 per year) for outsourced bookkeeping, versus EUR 18,000+ per year for a part-time in-house bookkeeper. The outsourced option is significantly cheaper for businesses below the threshold where a full-time bookkeeper is genuinely needed.

### When to Hire In-House

An in-house bookkeeper becomes cost-effective when your business processes more than 300 to 400 transactions per month, or when you need daily financial processing such as invoicing, payment processing, and bank reconciliation. At this volume, the monthly cost of an outsourced service approaches the cost of a part-time employee, and the in-house option provides greater availability and deeper integration with your operations.

Other triggers for hiring in-house include:

- You need someone to handle physical post, filing, and document management
- Your business requires daily cash handling or till reconciliation
- You have complex inventory management needs
- You want someone available for ad-hoc financial queries throughout the day

## Virtual Bookkeeping

Virtual bookkeeping -- where a bookkeeper works remotely using cloud accounting software -- has become the dominant model for small business bookkeeping in Ireland. The bookkeeper accesses your Xero, Sage, or QuickBooks account online, processes transactions, reconciles your bank feeds, and produces reports without ever visiting your premises.

### Advantages of Virtual Bookkeeping

- **Lower cost:** No overhead for desk space, equipment, or travel
- **Wider talent pool:** You can engage the best bookkeeper for your needs regardless of location
- **Real-time access:** Cloud software means both you and your bookkeeper see the same data simultaneously
- **Automated bank feeds:** Direct connections between your bank and accounting software reduce manual data entry
- **Scalability:** Easy to increase or decrease the service level as your business changes

### Typical Virtual Bookkeeping Costs

Virtual bookkeepers in Ireland typically charge 10 to 15 per cent less than on-site bookkeepers because they have lower overhead costs. A virtual bookkeeping service for a business with 100 to 150 transactions per month typically costs EUR 180 to EUR 300 per month.

### Software Requirements

To work with a virtual bookkeeper, you need cloud-based accounting software. The main options in Ireland are:

| Software | Monthly Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Xero | EUR 15-40 | Small to medium businesses, strong bank feeds |
| Sage Business Cloud | EUR 12-35 | Businesses familiar with Sage products |
| QuickBooks Online | EUR 15-35 | Sole traders and small companies |
| Surf Accounts | EUR 20-40 | Irish-specific features, CRO integration |

The software subscription is typically your cost, not the bookkeeper's. Some bookkeeping services include the software licence in their package fee, while others require you to maintain your own subscription and grant them access.

## What a Bookkeeper Does vs What an Accountant Does

Understanding the boundary between bookkeeping and accounting helps you engage the right professional at the right price for each type of work.

### Bookkeeper Responsibilities

- Recording daily transactions (sales, purchases, receipts, payments)
- Bank reconciliation
- Processing invoices and managing debtors/creditors
- Preparing VAT returns (calculations and data, not necessarily filing)
- Running payroll (data entry and calculations, not tax advisory)
- Producing basic financial reports
- Maintaining organised records for the accountant

### Accountant Responsibilities

- Preparing statutory financial statements
- Filing tax returns (Form 11, CT1) with Revenue
- Tax planning and advisory
- CRO annual return filing
- Audit and assurance (if required)
- Business structuring advice
- Revenue representation during audits or compliance checks

### Working Together

The most cost-effective model for small businesses is to engage a bookkeeper for the ongoing record-keeping work and an accountant for the annual compliance and advisory work. The bookkeeper handles the high-volume, lower-complexity work at EUR 25 to EUR 40 per hour. The accountant handles the lower-volume, higher-complexity work at EUR 100 to EUR 200 per hour.

This division of labour keeps your accountant's year-end fee down because they receive clean, reconciled records rather than raw data. A business that pays EUR 250 per month for a bookkeeper (EUR 3,000 per year) might save EUR 1,000 to EUR 2,000 on their annual accountant fee compared to having the accountant perform the bookkeeping as well.

## When to Hire a Bookkeeper

Not every business needs a dedicated bookkeeper. Here are the key indicators that it is time to engage one:

**You are spending more than five hours per week on bookkeeping.** If bookkeeping is consuming a significant portion of your working week, your time is almost certainly better spent on revenue-generating activities. At a bookkeeper rate of EUR 30 per hour, outsourcing five hours of weekly bookkeeping costs EUR 600 per month. If your time as a business owner is worth more than EUR 30 per hour in productive business activities, the maths works in favour of outsourcing.

**Your accountant is charging you for bookkeeping work.** If your accountant's year-end bill includes significant time for data entry, reconciliation, and record reconstruction, you are paying accountant rates (EUR 100-200/hour) for bookkeeper-level work (EUR 25-40/hour). Engaging a bookkeeper and providing your accountant with clean records is substantially cheaper.

**You are missing VAT deadlines or making errors.** Regular bookkeeping ensures your VAT returns are accurate and filed on time. Errors and late filings trigger penalties from Revenue, and the cost of those penalties quickly exceeds the cost of a bookkeeper.

**You have more than 50 transactions per month.** Below 50 transactions, most business owners can manage their own bookkeeping with basic software. Above 50, the time commitment becomes significant and the risk of errors increases. Above 150 transactions, dedicated bookkeeping support is almost essential.

**You are employing staff.** Payroll adds a layer of complexity that requires regular, accurate processing. PAYE, PRSI, USC, and LPT deductions must be calculated correctly and submitted to Revenue on time. A bookkeeper experienced in payroll can handle this as part of their monthly engagement.
