An accountant in Ireland typically costs between EUR 300 and EUR 800 per year for sole traders, and EUR 1,500 to EUR 3,500 per year for limited companies. The exact cost depends on your business structure, transaction volume, and whether you need additional services like VAT returns or payroll.

This guide breaks down typical accountant fees in Ireland so you know what to expect before you start getting quotes.

## Typical Fee Ranges

Accountant fees in Ireland depend primarily on whether you are a sole trader or a limited company, and on the volume of transactions your business processes each year.

### Sole Traders

Most sole traders in Ireland pay between **EUR 300 and EUR 800 per year** for a standard accounting package. This typically covers:

- Preparation and filing of your annual **Form 11** income tax return
- Calculation of income tax, USC, and PRSI liabilities
- Basic bookkeeping review or year-end accounts preparation
- Advice on allowable expenses and tax deductions

At the lower end of this range, you are likely working with a smaller practice or a sole practitioner. At the higher end, you may be getting more hands-on bookkeeping support or have a more complex tax situation (rental income, foreign income, capital gains).

### Limited Companies

Limited company accounting is substantially more involved, and fees reflect this. Most small limited companies in Ireland pay between **EUR 1,500 and EUR 3,500 per year**. This typically includes:

- Preparation of annual financial statements (profit and loss, balance sheet)
- Filing the **Form CT1** corporation tax return
- Filing the **annual return (B1 form)** with the CRO
- Preparation of abridged accounts for CRO filing
- Director's personal Form 11 return
- Payroll processing (if the company has employees or pays directors a salary)
- VAT return preparation (if VAT-registered)

Companies with higher turnover, multiple employees, or complex structures (group companies, holding structures) can expect fees at the upper end or beyond this range.

### Startups

If you are just starting out and have minimal transactions, some accountants offer startup packages from **EUR 500 to EUR 1,200** for the first year. These often include company formation, initial Revenue registrations (corporation tax, PAYE, VAT), and the first set of annual accounts.

## What Affects the Cost

Several factors influence what an accountant will charge:

- **Business structure:** Limited companies cost more than sole traders due to additional CRO filings and statutory requirements
- **Transaction volume:** More invoices, receipts, and bank transactions mean more bookkeeping time
- **Record quality:** If you hand over a shoebox of receipts, expect to pay significantly more than if you maintain organised digital records or use bookkeeping software
- **VAT registration:** Filing bi-monthly VAT3 returns adds to the workload
- **Payroll:** Processing monthly payroll, P30 returns, and year-end P35 filings is an additional service
- **Advisory services:** Tax planning, Revenue audit support, and business advice are typically charged separately, either hourly or as part of a premium package

## How to Compare Quotes

When getting quotes from accountants, make sure you are comparing like with like. Ask each accountant to specify:

1. **What is included** in the quoted fee (tax return, accounts, CRO filings, payroll, VAT)
2. **What is charged separately** (ad-hoc queries, tax planning, Revenue correspondence)
3. **How additional work is billed** (hourly rate, fixed fee per task, or included within a capped monthly retainer)
4. **Whether the fee is fixed or estimated** -- some accountants quote a low base fee and then add charges for every phone call or email

A clear, fixed-fee quote with a defined scope of work is generally preferable to an open-ended hourly arrangement, especially for small businesses with predictable needs.

## When to Hire an Accountant

You are not legally required to use an accountant in Ireland. Sole traders and company directors can prepare and file their own returns. However, most business owners find that a good accountant pays for themselves through:

- **Tax savings:** Identifying allowable expenses you might miss (home office costs, motor expenses, pension contributions)
- **Compliance:** Ensuring you meet Revenue and CRO deadlines, avoiding late filing penalties (which can be EUR 100 per day for CRO annual returns)
- **Time savings:** Freeing you to focus on running your business rather than wrestling with tax forms
- **Peace of mind:** Knowing your affairs are in order if Revenue selects you for audit

If your business is straightforward -- a sole trader with one income source and minimal expenses -- you may manage your own Form 11 using ROS. But the moment you incorporate, hire staff, register for VAT, or earn above EUR 50,000, professional accounting support becomes a practical necessity.

## Summary

Accountant fees in Ireland range from EUR 300 to EUR 800 for sole traders and EUR 1,500 to EUR 3,500 for limited companies. The exact cost depends on your business complexity, record-keeping quality, and the services you need. Always get itemised quotes from at least two or three accountants, compare what is included, and choose someone who understands your industry and communicates clearly.
