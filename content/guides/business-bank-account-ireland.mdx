To open a business bank account in Ireland, you need to choose a bank, provide proof of identity and address, supply your business registration documents, and complete the bank's application process. For sole traders, the process typically takes 1 to 3 weeks with a traditional bank, while digital-first banks like Revolut Business can often have you set up within days.

While there is no legal requirement for sole traders to hold a separate business bank account in Ireland, separating your business and personal finances is one of the most important administrative decisions you will make. It simplifies bookkeeping, makes tax returns straightforward, provides a clear audit trail for Revenue, and projects professionalism to clients and suppliers. For limited companies, a separate business account is effectively mandatory because the company is a distinct legal entity from its directors.

## Why You Need a Separate Business Account

The reasons for maintaining a separate business bank account go beyond simple tidiness. Here is why it matters for Irish businesses specifically.

### Tax Compliance and Revenue Audits

Revenue Commissioners can request your bank statements during an audit. If your business and personal transactions are mixed in a single account, every transaction must be explained and categorised. This wastes time, increases your accountant's fees, and raises the risk of errors or omissions that could trigger penalties.

With a dedicated business account, your income and expenses are clearly separated. Your accountant can reconcile your bank statements against your invoices efficiently, and Revenue auditors can verify your records without wading through personal transactions.

### Cleaner Bookkeeping

Whether you use accounting software such as Sage, Xero, or QuickBooks, or manage your books through a spreadsheet, a dedicated business account makes reconciliation straightforward. You can connect your business account directly to your accounting software for automatic bank feeds, eliminating manual data entry and reducing the chance of missed transactions.

### Professional Image

Paying suppliers, receiving client payments, and issuing refunds from a dedicated business account signals that you run a serious operation. Some clients, particularly larger companies and public sector bodies, may specifically require payment to or from a business account.

### Loan and Grant Applications

If you apply for a business loan from your bank, a Local Enterprise Office (LEO) grant, or Microfinance Ireland funding, you will need to provide business bank statements showing your trading history, cash flow, and financial management. A separate account makes this documentation clean and compelling.

## Irish Banks Offering Business Accounts

The business banking landscape in Ireland has changed significantly in recent years, particularly following the exits of Ulster Bank and KBC. Here is what is currently available.

### AIB Business Banking

AIB is one of the two largest business banks in Ireland. They offer a range of business current accounts suited to different business sizes.

- **Fee-free banking:** AIB offers 18 months of fee-free banking for new business customers, after which standard transaction fees apply
- **Online and mobile banking:** Full online banking with business-specific features including bulk payments and payroll integration
- **Overdraft facilities:** Available subject to credit approval
- **Business credit cards:** Visa business cards with expense management features
- **Branch network:** Extensive branch coverage across Ireland for in-person banking needs

Transaction fees after the free period typically include a maintenance fee per quarter, charges per transaction (lodgements, payments, direct debits), and ATM withdrawal fees.

### Bank of Ireland Business Banking

Bank of Ireland is the other major pillar bank offering comprehensive business banking services.

- **Startup account:** Reduced fees for the first 2 years of trading
- **365 Online:** Full online and mobile banking platform
- **Merchant services:** Card payment terminals and e-commerce payment solutions
- **Trade finance:** Letters of credit and trade guarantees for importing/exporting businesses
- **Relationship managers:** Dedicated business banking managers for growing businesses

Bank of Ireland has invested heavily in its digital business banking platform, and most routine transactions can be handled online without visiting a branch.

### Permanent TSB Business Banking

Permanent TSB offers business banking services, though their business product range is smaller than AIB or Bank of Ireland.

- **Business current account:** Standard transactional banking
- **Online banking:** Basic online banking facilities
- **Business lending:** Loans and overdraft facilities
- **More limited branch network:** Fewer business-specialist branches than the pillar banks

PTSB may suit sole traders or small businesses with straightforward banking needs who value personal service at their local branch.

### Revolut Business

Revolut Business has become increasingly popular with Irish small businesses and sole traders. It operates as an electronic money institution rather than a traditional bank.

- **Quick setup:** Account opening can be completed online in minutes with digital identity verification
- **Low fees:** No monthly fees on the basic plan; premium plans with additional features available
- **Multi-currency:** Hold and transact in multiple currencies with competitive exchange rates -- useful for businesses with international clients
- **Expense management:** Built-in expense tracking, receipt capture, and team card management
- **Integrations:** Connects with accounting software including Xero and QuickBooks
- **No branch network:** Entirely digital -- no physical branches

Revolut Business accounts are protected by the Central Bank of Lithuania (where Revolut holds its banking licence) rather than the Central Bank of Ireland. This is an important distinction for businesses holding significant balances.

### N26 Business

N26 offers a business account for freelancers and self-employed individuals (sole traders). It is a German-licensed bank supervised by the European Central Bank.

- **Free basic account:** No monthly fees for the standard business account
- **Quick digital setup:** Online application with video verification
- **Cashback:** 0.1 per cent cashback on qualifying purchases
- **Sub-accounts (Spaces):** Separate pots for tax savings, VAT, and other purposes
- **Limited business features:** No joint accounts, no business lending, no merchant services

N26 Business is best suited to sole traders and freelancers who want a simple, low-cost digital account and do not need traditional business banking services such as overdrafts or merchant terminals.

### An Post Money (Current Account)

An Post, in partnership with various banking providers, offers current account services. While not specifically marketed as a business account, some sole traders use An Post current accounts for business purposes due to the extensive post office network across Ireland, particularly in rural areas.

## Documents Required to Open a Business Account

The documentation requirements vary depending on your business structure and the bank you choose, but generally you will need the following.

### For Sole Traders

- **Proof of identity:** Valid passport or Irish driving licence
- **Proof of address:** Utility bill, bank statement, or Revenue correspondence dated within the last 6 months
- **Revenue registration confirmation:** Your TR1 registration acknowledgement or tax registration certificate
- **Certificate of Business Name Registration:** If trading under a name other than your own (from the CRO)
- **Description of business activity:** A brief summary of what your business does, who your customers are, and your expected turnover

### For Limited Companies

- **Certificate of Incorporation:** Issued by the CRO when your company was formed
- **Memorandum and Articles of Association:** Your company's constitution document
- **CRO annual return confirmation:** Evidence that the company is in good standing
- **Directors' identification:** Proof of identity and address for all directors
- **Board resolution:** A resolution authorising the opening of the bank account and specifying who can operate it
- **Tax registration confirmation:** Company tax registration from Revenue
- **Beneficial ownership details:** Information about anyone who owns or controls more than 25 per cent of the company (Anti-Money Laundering requirement)

### For Partnerships

- **Partnership agreement:** The formal agreement between partners
- **Proof of identity and address:** For all partners
- **Revenue registration confirmation:** Partnership tax reference number
- **Certificate of Business Name Registration:** From the CRO

## Fees Comparison

Business banking fees in Ireland can add up, especially for businesses that process many transactions. Here is a general comparison of the fee structures.

| Bank | Monthly/Quarterly Fee | Transaction Fees | Free Banking Period |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| AIB | Quarterly maintenance fee | Per transaction charges | 18 months |
| Bank of Ireland | Quarterly maintenance fee | Per transaction charges | Up to 2 years (startup) |
| Permanent TSB | Quarterly maintenance fee | Per transaction charges | Varies |
| Revolut Business | Free (basic) / EUR 25+ (premium) | Included in plan limits | Ongoing (basic plan) |
| N26 Business | Free (standard) | Free standard transfers | Ongoing |

Transaction fees at traditional banks typically range from EUR 0.20 to EUR 0.35 per electronic transaction and EUR 0.50 or more per cash or cheque lodgement item. Quarterly maintenance fees range from approximately EUR 15 to EUR 30. These fees are tax-deductible as a business expense.

## Online-Only vs Traditional Banks

The choice between a digital-first bank and a traditional bank depends on your business needs.

### Choose a Traditional Bank If You

- Handle cash frequently (retail, hospitality, trades)
- Need an overdraft facility or business loan
- Want merchant services (card payment terminals)
- Prefer face-to-face banking with a relationship manager
- Need trade finance or letter of credit facilities
- Want deposit protection under the Central Bank of Ireland scheme

### Choose a Digital Bank If You

- Operate primarily online with electronic payments
- Want minimal fees and fast account setup
- Have international clients and need multi-currency support
- Prefer managing everything through a mobile app
- Do not need overdraft or lending facilities
- Are comfortable with customer support via chat or email only

Many Irish businesses use both -- a traditional bank account for core business banking and lending, and a digital account for international payments, expense management, or specific projects.

## How Long Does It Take to Open an Account?

The timeline varies significantly between providers.

- **Revolut Business / N26:** Same day to 3 business days (digital verification)
- **AIB / Bank of Ireland / PTSB:** 1 to 3 weeks for straightforward applications; longer if additional documentation is requested
- **Complex applications:** Companies with multiple directors, non-resident directors, or complex ownership structures may take 4 to 6 weeks

To speed up the process with a traditional bank, book an appointment with a business banking adviser, bring all required documents to the first meeting, and ensure all directors or signatories are available if needed.

## Common Issues and Solutions

**Application rejected or delayed.** Banks must comply with Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations and may request additional documentation about your business activities, source of funds, or beneficial ownership. Provide clear, honest answers and supply any requested documents promptly.

**Non-resident directors.** If your company has directors who are not resident in Ireland, traditional banks may require additional verification steps. This can extend the application timeline significantly. Digital banks often handle this more efficiently.

**Industry restrictions.** Some banks are reluctant to open accounts for businesses in certain industries, such as cryptocurrency, gambling, or adult entertainment. If your business falls into a restricted category, you may need to approach multiple banks or consider a specialist provider.

**Switching banks.** If you are moving your business account from one bank to another (for example, following the Ulster Bank or KBC closures), the Central Bank of Ireland's switching code provides a structured process. Your new bank should facilitate the transfer of standing orders, direct debits, and regular payments within 10 working days.

## Setting Up Your Account for Success

Once your business bank account is open, take these steps to get the most from it.

**Connect to accounting software.** Set up bank feeds with your accounting software immediately. This automates transaction imports and saves hours of manual data entry each month.

**Set up tax savings transfers.** Create a standing order to transfer a percentage of each payment received (typically 25 to 35 per cent) into a separate savings account for tax. This ensures you always have funds available for your preliminary tax payment and any VAT due.

**Register for online banking.** Ensure you have full online access set up, including the ability to make payments, view statements, and manage direct debits. For companies, ensure all authorised signatories have their own login credentials.

**Order a business debit card.** Use this for all business purchases to maintain a clear trail. Avoid using personal cards for business expenses where possible.

**Set up direct debits for regular payments.** Insurance premiums, software subscriptions, and utility bills should be paid by direct debit from your business account to maintain clean records.
