Setting up a limited company in Ireland costs between EUR 300 and EUR 600 in total when using a formation agent, or as little as EUR 50 if you file directly with the Companies Registration Office (CRO) yourself. The CRO registration fee is EUR 50 for online filing and EUR 100 for paper filing. Beyond this statutory fee, the total cost depends on whether you use a formation agent, an accountant, or a solicitor to handle the process, and what additional services you need in your first year of operation.

This guide breaks down every cost involved in forming an Irish limited company, from the initial CRO filing through to the ongoing expenses you will face in your first twelve months of trading.

## CRO Registration Fee

The Companies Registration Office is the statutory body responsible for company incorporation in Ireland. Every new company must be registered with the CRO through their online portal, CORE (Companies Online Registration Environment).

### Filing Fees

| Filing Method | Fee | Processing Time |
|---|---|---|
| Online via CORE | EUR 50 | 5-10 working days |
| Paper filing (Form A1) | EUR 100 | 10-15 working days |
| Express online processing | EUR 50 + EUR 100 = EUR 150 | 2 working days |

The online filing fee of EUR 50 is the standard route for most new companies. Paper filing is rarely used now except in unusual circumstances. Express processing adds EUR 100 to the standard fee and guarantees registration within two working days -- useful if you need the company set up urgently for a contract or bank account.

### What the CRO Fee Covers

The registration fee covers:

- Processing your incorporation documents
- Issuing your company's Certificate of Incorporation
- Assigning your unique company registration number
- Entering the company on the public register
- Making your company's details available on the CRO's online search facility

The CRO fee does not include preparation of the required documents (constitution, Form A1, consent forms). These must be prepared separately, either by you, a formation agent, or a professional advisor.

## Formation Agent Fees

A company formation agent handles the administrative process of setting up your company with the CRO. They prepare all required documents, file them electronically, and liaise with the CRO until registration is complete.

### What Formation Agents Charge

| Service Level | Fee (excl. CRO fee) | What Is Included |
|---|---|---|
| Basic formation | EUR 150-250 | Standard constitution, Form A1 filing, Certificate of Incorporation |
| Standard formation | EUR 250-350 | Basic plus registered office address (1 year), company seal, statutory registers |
| Premium formation | EUR 350-500+ | Standard plus Revenue registration, bank account assistance, share certificates |

### What Is Included in a Standard Formation Package

A typical formation agent package at EUR 250 to EUR 350 includes:

- **Preparation of the company constitution.** Since the Companies Act 2014, all new private limited companies (LTDs) are formed with a single-document constitution. The agent prepares this using a standard template that covers the company's objects, share structure, and internal governance rules.

- **Filing the Form A1.** The incorporation form submitted to the CRO containing details of the company name, registered office, directors, secretary, share capital, and activity classification (NACE code).

- **Director and secretary consent forms.** Written consents from each proposed director and the company secretary confirming their willingness to act.

- **Registered office address (first year).** Many formation agents provide a registered office address service. Your registered office is the official address where legal and statutory correspondence is delivered. It appears on the public CRO register. If you do not want to use your home address, a registered office service costs EUR 100 to EUR 200 per year.

- **Company seal.** Since the Companies Act 2014, a company seal is no longer mandatory for private limited companies. However, some agents still include one in their package. If purchased separately, a company seal costs EUR 30 to EUR 60.

- **Basic statutory registers.** Template statutory registers for members, directors, secretaries, and charges. These are legally required under the Companies Act 2014 and must be maintained throughout the company's life.

### DIY Formation via CORE

You can form a company yourself through the CRO's CORE system. This saves the formation agent fee but requires you to:

1. Prepare the company constitution (templates are available from the CRO website and various legal resources)
2. Complete the Form A1 online
3. Obtain and upload director and secretary consent forms
4. Have the constitution signed by the subscribers (founding shareholders)
5. Pay the EUR 50 online filing fee

DIY formation is feasible if you are forming a standard private company limited by shares (LTD) with a simple structure. If your company has a complex share structure, multiple classes of shares, or bespoke constitutional provisions, professional assistance is advisable.

The CORE system provides step-by-step guidance, and the CRO publishes information notes explaining the requirements. The most common reasons for rejection are errors in the Form A1 (incorrect NACE codes, missing information) or issues with the constitution (formatting, missing required clauses).

## Accountant Formation Packages

Many accountants offer company formation as part of a first-year startup package. This is often the best value option because it combines formation with the essential accounting setup you will need immediately.

### Typical Accountant Startup Packages

| Package | Fee | What Is Included |
|---|---|---|
| Formation only | EUR 250-450 | Company formation, CRO registration, basic setup |
| First-year startup | EUR 500-1,200 | Formation + Revenue registration + first year accounts + CT1 + director's Form 11 |
| Comprehensive startup | EUR 1,200-2,000 | All of the above + payroll setup + VAT registration + bookkeeping setup + quarterly reviews |

The first-year startup package at EUR 500 to EUR 1,200 is the most popular choice. It represents a discount of 30 to 50 per cent on the cost of purchasing each service separately and ensures that your company's tax affairs are properly established from day one.

### Why Accountants Offer Startup Discounts

Startup packages are loss leaders. The accountant charges below their normal rates in the first year to secure a long-term client relationship. The expectation is that you will continue using them for annual compliance work at their standard rates from year two onwards.

This is not a negative -- it genuinely benefits both parties. You get professional setup at a reduced cost, and the accountant gains a client they expect to serve for many years. The key is to understand that your fees will increase from year two, so budget accordingly.

## Post-Incorporation Costs

Forming the company is only the beginning. In your first year, you will face several additional costs that are essential for operating legally.

### Revenue Registration

After incorporation, you must register with Revenue for the taxes relevant to your business:

- **Corporation tax** registration is mandatory for all companies
- **PAYE/PRSI** registration is required if you will pay salaries (including director salaries)
- **VAT** registration is required if your turnover exceeds or is likely to exceed the VAT thresholds (EUR 80,000 for goods, EUR 40,000 for services)

Revenue registration itself is free. The cost is the professional time to complete the registration forms (Form TR2 for companies), which is typically EUR 100 to EUR 200 if done by your accountant, or free if included in a startup package.

### CRO Annual Return

Every company must file an annual return with the CRO. The filing fee is EUR 20 per year. Your first annual return date is set six months after incorporation.

Missing the annual return deadline triggers penalties of EUR 100 per day up to EUR 5,000, plus the loss of audit exemption. These penalties are severe relative to the EUR 20 filing fee, making timely compliance essential.

### Registered Office

If you use your home address as the registered office, there is no additional cost. However, your home address will appear on the public CRO register and in the company's abridged accounts.

If you prefer privacy, a registered office service costs EUR 100 to EUR 300 per year. This provides a commercial address for CRO correspondence and public filings while keeping your home address off the register.

### Business Bank Account

Irish banks charge varying fees for business current accounts:

| Bank | Monthly Fee | Transaction Charges |
|---|---|---|
| AIB | EUR 0-7 | Per-transaction charges apply |
| Bank of Ireland | EUR 0-6 | Per-transaction charges apply |
| Permanent TSB | EUR 0-6 | Per-transaction charges apply |
| Revolut Business | EUR 0-25 | Free transactions on some plans |
| N26 Business | EUR 0 | Limited features for Irish companies |

Most traditional banks offer free banking for the first six to twelve months for new companies. After the introductory period, fees typically range from EUR 5 to EUR 15 per month plus per-transaction charges.

Opening a business bank account requires your Certificate of Incorporation, company constitution, proof of identity and address for all directors, and sometimes a letter from your accountant confirming the nature of your business.

### Professional Indemnity and Public Liability Insurance

Depending on your sector, you may need:

- **Professional indemnity insurance:** EUR 300 to EUR 800 per year for professional services companies
- **Public liability insurance:** EUR 300 to EUR 1,000 per year depending on your business activities
- **Employer liability insurance:** Required if you have employees, typically EUR 500 to EUR 1,500 per year

### Accounting Software

Setting up a cloud accounting system costs EUR 15 to EUR 40 per month (EUR 180 to EUR 480 per year) for platforms like Xero, Sage, or QuickBooks. This is essential for maintaining proper records from day one.

## Total First-Year Cost Breakdown

Here is a realistic breakdown of what forming and running a limited company costs in the first year:

| Item | Low Estimate | High Estimate |
|---|---|---|
| CRO registration fee | EUR 50 | EUR 50 |
| Formation agent / accountant | EUR 150 | EUR 500 |
| Revenue registration | EUR 0 (DIY) | EUR 200 |
| Registered office (if needed) | EUR 0 | EUR 300 |
| Business bank account | EUR 0 (free period) | EUR 180 |
| Accounting software | EUR 180 | EUR 480 |
| Accountant annual fee (year 1) | EUR 500 | EUR 1,500 |
| CRO annual return | EUR 20 | EUR 20 |
| Insurance | EUR 300 | EUR 1,500 |
| **Total first year** | **EUR 1,200** | **EUR 4,730** |

The low estimate assumes a DIY-oriented founder who handles their own formation and basic bookkeeping, with a minimal accountant engagement. The high estimate represents a fully serviced setup with professional support at each stage.

## Comparing Formation Agents

When choosing a formation agent, compare:

**Price.** Formation fees vary significantly for essentially the same service. Get quotes from at least three providers.

**Turnaround time.** Standard processing takes 5 to 10 working days. If you need faster turnaround, confirm whether the agent offers express processing and what the additional cost is.

**Post-formation support.** Some agents provide ongoing support (company secretarial services, registered office, mail forwarding) while others handle only the formation. If you need ongoing services, a bundled arrangement may be better value.

**Reputation and reviews.** Check online reviews and ask for recommendations from other business owners or your accountant. The formation process is straightforward, but errors cause delays and potential rejection by the CRO.

**Transparency.** Ensure the quoted fee includes all costs, including the CRO filing fee, VAT on the agent's fee, and any disbursements. Some agents advertise low headline prices and add charges for items that others include as standard.

Popular formation agents in Ireland include Company Bureau, CompanyFormations.ie, and various accountancy firms offering formation as part of their service offering. Prices typically range from EUR 99 to EUR 400 plus the CRO fee.
