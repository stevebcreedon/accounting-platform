Irish businesses can access a range of tax credits and reliefs that significantly reduce their tax bills. The R&D tax credit alone provides a 25 per cent credit on qualifying research expenditure, and start-up company relief can eliminate corporation tax entirely for the first three years of trading. Combined with capital allowances, employment incentives, and loss relief provisions, these reliefs form a substantial support system for Irish businesses.

Many business owners leave money on the table by not claiming reliefs they are entitled to. Revenue does not automatically apply these credits. You must identify the reliefs relevant to your business, meet the qualifying conditions, and make a formal claim on your tax return. This guide covers the most important tax credits and reliefs available to Irish businesses, whether you operate as a sole trader or through a limited company.

## R&D Tax Credit

The Research and Development (R&D) tax credit is one of Ireland's most valuable business reliefs. It provides a 25 per cent tax credit on qualifying R&D expenditure, which can be used to reduce your corporation tax liability or, if the credit exceeds the liability, converted to a cash refund.

### What Qualifies as R&D?

To claim the R&D tax credit, your activities must meet Revenue's definition of research and development. The activities must:

- Involve systematic, investigative, or experimental activities
- Seek to achieve scientific or technological advancement
- Involve the resolution of scientific or technological uncertainty

The key word is uncertainty. If the outcome of the work can be readily determined by a competent professional in the relevant field, it does not qualify. The advancement must be in the overall state of knowledge, not just new to your company.

### What Does Not Qualify

Routine testing, quality control, market research, commercial and financial steps, arts and humanities research, and social science research do not qualify. Revenue is particularly strict about distinguishing routine development work from genuine R&D. Customising existing software for a client, for example, would generally not qualify unless it involves resolving a genuine technological uncertainty.

### Qualifying Expenditure

The credit is calculated on the following qualifying expenditure:

**Staff costs:** Salaries, wages, and employer PRSI for employees directly engaged in R&D activities. This is typically the largest component of an R&D claim. You must be able to demonstrate what proportion of each employee's time was spent on qualifying R&D.

**Materials:** The cost of materials consumed or transformed in R&D activities. This does not include materials used in normal production.

**Overheads:** A proportion of overhead costs (heat, light, power, rent) attributable to R&D activities. Revenue accepts a reasonable apportionment method.

**Subcontracted R&D:** Expenditure on R&D activities subcontracted to universities, institutes of higher education, or other companies. Subcontracted expenditure is subject to limits (generally 15 per cent of total qualifying expenditure, with a higher limit for university subcontracting).

### Calculating the Credit

The R&D tax credit is 25 per cent of qualifying expenditure. For a company that incurs EUR 200,000 in qualifying R&D expenditure:

- R&D tax credit: EUR 200,000 x 25% = EUR 50,000

### Using the Credit

The credit is first applied to reduce the company's corporation tax liability for the accounting period. If the credit exceeds the liability (which is common for early-stage companies with limited profits), the excess can be:

1. **Carried back** to the preceding accounting period
2. **Paid as a cash refund** over 33 months (three instalments)
3. **Carried forward** indefinitely for use against future corporation tax

The cash refund option is particularly valuable for pre-profit companies. The first instalment (one-third of the excess credit) is paid 12 months after the end of the accounting period, the second at 24 months, and the third at 33 months.

### Key Employee Provision

Companies can allocate part of their R&D tax credit to key employees who have been directly involved in R&D activities. The allocated credit is treated as a credit against the employee's income tax liability. This provision can be used as a retention tool for valuable R&D staff.

### Making a Claim

R&D tax credit claims are made on the CT1 corporation tax return. You must maintain contemporaneous records that demonstrate the R&D activities carried out, the scientific or technological uncertainty addressed, and the expenditure incurred. Revenue can request this documentation during a compliance check, and claims without adequate records can be denied.

## Start-Up Company Relief

Start-up company relief provides a three-year exemption from corporation tax for new companies that begin a qualifying trade. The relief is designed to support new businesses in their critical early years.

### Eligibility

To qualify, the company must:

- Be a new company (not a successor to an existing trade or formed by splitting an existing company)
- Commence a qualifying trade (most active trading activities qualify)
- Have a corporation tax liability of EUR 40,000 or less in each of the first three accounting periods

### How the Relief Works

The relief operates through a credit against corporation tax equal to the employer PRSI paid by the company, up to a maximum of EUR 40,000 per accounting period. If the company's corporation tax liability is EUR 30,000 and it paid EUR 35,000 in employer PRSI, the full EUR 30,000 of corporation tax is eliminated.

### Marginal Relief

If the corporation tax liability is between EUR 40,000 and EUR 60,000, marginal relief applies. The relief is reduced so that companies with liabilities between these thresholds receive partial relief.

### Carry Forward

If the relief exceeds the corporation tax liability in any of the first three years, the unused portion can be carried forward to subsequent years. This means that even if the company does not have a tax liability in the first three years, the relief is not lost.

### Important Considerations

Start-up relief does not exempt the company from filing CT1 returns. You must still file on time and make claims for the relief on the return. The relief applies only to corporation tax, not to other taxes such as VAT, PAYE, or employer PRSI.

## Capital Allowances

Capital allowances provide tax relief for the cost of acquiring business assets. Instead of deducting the full cost in the year of purchase (which is not permitted), the cost is written off over a period of years through annual capital allowances.

### Plant and Machinery: 12.5 Per Cent Over 8 Years

The standard capital allowance rate for plant and machinery is 12.5 per cent per annum on a straight-line basis. This means you deduct 12.5 per cent of the cost each year for 8 years, after which the full cost has been written off against your taxable profits.

Plant and machinery includes computers, office equipment, furniture, tools, manufacturing equipment, and most other tangible assets used in the business. The key test is whether the item is used for the purposes of the trade.

### Motor Vehicles

Capital allowances on motor vehicles are restricted. The maximum cost eligible for capital allowances on a car is EUR 24,000, regardless of the actual purchase price. The annual allowance is 12.5 per cent of the qualifying cost (maximum EUR 3,000 per year). If the car is used partly for personal purposes, the allowance is further restricted proportionally.

Commercial vehicles (vans, trucks) are not subject to the EUR 24,000 limit and qualify for capital allowances on the full cost.

### Industrial Buildings: 4 Per Cent Over 25 Years

Industrial buildings used for manufacturing, certain tourism operations, and other qualifying purposes attract an annual capital allowance of 4 per cent over 25 years. Hotels, holiday camps, and registered nursing homes have different rates and conditions.

### Accelerated Capital Allowances for Energy-Efficient Equipment

Businesses that purchase qualifying energy-efficient equipment can claim 100 per cent of the cost as a capital allowance in the first year, instead of spreading it over 8 years. This is a significant cash flow benefit and an incentive to invest in energy-efficient technology.

Qualifying equipment must be from a category listed on the Sustainable Energy Authority of Ireland (SEAI) Triple E Register, which includes energy-efficient motors, lighting, heating systems, building energy management systems, and certain renewable energy equipment.

### Claiming Capital Allowances

Capital allowances are claimed on your annual tax return (Form 11 for sole traders, CT1 for companies). You must maintain records of the asset cost, date of purchase, business use percentage, and annual allowances claimed. Revenue can disallow claims where records are inadequate or where the asset is not used for business purposes.

## Employment Investment Incentive (EIIS)

The Employment Investment Incentive (EIIS) is a tax relief that encourages individuals to invest in small and medium enterprises (SMEs) by providing income tax relief on the investment amount.

### How EIIS Works

Investors who subscribe for new ordinary shares in a qualifying company can claim income tax relief at 40 per cent of the amount invested (30 per cent initial relief plus 10 per cent follow-on relief after the holding period). The maximum annual investment eligible for relief is EUR 250,000 for employees of the company and EUR 500,000 for external investors.

### Qualifying Companies

To qualify, the company must be a micro, small, or medium enterprise within the EU definition. It must carry on a qualifying trade, be incorporated in Ireland or the EEA, and have its main activities based in Ireland. Certain activities are excluded, including professional services, financial services, and property development.

### Benefit to the Company

While EIIS is primarily a relief for the investor, it benefits the company by making it easier to attract equity investment. Investors are willing to accept higher risk because the tax relief effectively reduces their cost of investment. The company receives the full investment amount as share capital.

## Key Employee Engagement Programme (KEEP)

KEEP is a share option scheme that allows qualifying companies to grant share options to employees on favourable tax terms. Under KEEP, employees who exercise share options pay CGT at 33 per cent on the gain when they sell the shares, rather than income tax, USC, and PRSI at the time of exercise (which could total over 50 per cent).

### Qualifying Conditions

The company must be a qualifying SME with a market value of EUR 3 million or less at the time the option is granted. The employee must work at least 30 hours per week for the company. The total value of shares under KEEP options cannot exceed EUR 6 million per company, and the total value of options granted to any individual cannot exceed EUR 300,000.

### Why KEEP Matters

For start-ups and growing companies competing for talent against larger firms with higher salary budgets, KEEP provides a way to incentivise employees through equity participation without creating an immediate tax liability. The employee shares in the company's growth, and the favourable CGT treatment makes the option genuinely valuable.

## Trading Losses Relief

Losses incurred in the course of a trade can be used to reduce your tax liability, either in the current period or in future periods.

### For Sole Traders

Self-employed individuals can set trading losses against their total income for the same tax year. If the loss exceeds total income, the excess can be carried forward against future profits from the same trade. In the early years of a new business, losses can also be carried back to the preceding year, which can generate a tax refund.

### For Companies

Companies can carry forward trading losses indefinitely against future profits from the same trade. Losses can also be carried back to the immediately preceding accounting period. Group relief allows losses to be surrendered between companies in the same 75 per cent group.

### Value of Loss Relief

Loss relief is often undervalued by business owners. A trading loss of EUR 50,000 for a self-employed person paying tax at the marginal rate of 40 per cent (plus USC and PRSI) effectively saves over EUR 25,000 in taxes. For companies, a EUR 50,000 loss saves EUR 6,250 in corporation tax at the 12.5 per cent rate.

## Other Business Reliefs

Several other reliefs may be relevant depending on your specific circumstances.

### Film Relief (Section 481)

Companies producing qualifying films, television programmes, or animations in Ireland can claim a corporation tax credit of 32 per cent (or 37 per cent for projects in certain regions) of eligible expenditure, up to EUR 70 million per project.

### Transfer of Business to a Company

When a sole trader incorporates their business, the transfer of assets to the company can be structured to defer any CGT that would otherwise arise. Relief under Section 600 TCA 1997 rolls the gain into the shares received in the new company.

### Stamp Duty Reliefs

Various stamp duty reliefs apply to business transactions, including reduced rates for commercial property, group relief for transfers within connected companies, and exemptions for certain types of intellectual property.

### Double Taxation Relief

Irish businesses with income from foreign sources can claim double taxation relief to avoid being taxed twice on the same income. Ireland has an extensive network of double taxation agreements with over 70 countries, and unilateral relief is available where no agreement exists.

## How to Claim Tax Credits and Reliefs

Tax credits and reliefs are claimed on your annual tax return. The process differs depending on whether you are a sole trader or a company.

### Sole Traders (Form 11)

Self-employed individuals claim reliefs on the annual Form 11 income tax return. This includes capital allowances, loss relief, personal tax credits, and the earned income tax credit. The Form 11 is due by 31 October of the following year (mid-November via ROS).

### Companies (CT1)

Companies claim reliefs on the annual CT1 corporation tax return. This includes the R&D tax credit, start-up company relief, capital allowances, loss relief, and group relief. The CT1 is due within 9 months of the accounting period end.

### Record Keeping

Revenue can request documentation to support any relief claimed. Maintain detailed records including invoices, contracts, time records (for R&D claims), and calculations showing how each relief was computed. Records must be retained for at least 6 years after the end of the tax year in which the claim was made.

### Professional Advice

Many of these reliefs have complex qualifying conditions and interaction effects. Claiming one relief may affect your eligibility for another. A qualified accountant who understands the Irish tax system can identify all available reliefs and structure your claims to maximise the total benefit.

## Summary

Irish businesses have access to a comprehensive suite of tax credits and reliefs that can substantially reduce their tax bills. The R&D tax credit (25 per cent of qualifying expenditure), start-up company relief (three-year corporation tax exemption), and capital allowances (12.5 per cent per annum for plant and machinery) are the three most commonly claimed reliefs.

Employment-related incentives like EIIS and KEEP help companies attract investment and talent. Loss relief provisions allow businesses to smooth their tax liability over profitable and loss-making years. Accelerated capital allowances for energy-efficient equipment provide both a tax benefit and an environmental incentive.

The key to benefiting from these reliefs is awareness and planning. Many reliefs require advance planning to meet qualifying conditions, and all require proper documentation and timely claims on your tax return. Revenue does not offer these reliefs automatically. You must claim them.
