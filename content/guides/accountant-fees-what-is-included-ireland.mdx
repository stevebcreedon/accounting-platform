Irish accountants typically include year-end accounts preparation, tax return filing, and a basic review meeting in their standard fee. However, what is actually included varies significantly between firms, and the difference between a EUR 1,500 and a EUR 3,000 quote often comes down to scope rather than quality. This guide explains exactly what standard and premium accounting packages include, identifies common extras that are charged separately, highlights hidden charges to watch for, and shows you how to compare packages effectively.

Understanding what you are paying for is the single most important step in getting value from your accountant. Too many Irish business owners accept a headline figure without asking what it covers, then face unexpected invoices for services they assumed were included.

## Standard Package: What Is Typically Included

A standard accounting package in Ireland covers the essential compliance work that Revenue and the CRO require. The exact inclusions depend on whether you are a sole trader or a limited company.

### Sole Trader Standard Package (EUR 300-800/year)

A typical sole trader package includes:

- **Form 11 preparation and filing.** Your accountant prepares your annual income tax return, entering all income, expenses, and reliefs across the relevant sections of the Form 11, and files it via ROS before the deadline.

- **Tax liability calculation.** Calculation of your income tax at the standard (20%) and higher (40%) rates, plus Universal Social Charge and PRSI. This includes calculating your preliminary tax payment for the current year.

- **Trading accounts preparation.** A profit and loss account for your business showing total income, allowable expenses by category, and net trading profit. This forms the basis of the income figures entered on your Form 11.

- **Basic capital allowances.** Calculation of wear and tear allowances on business assets (vehicles, equipment, furniture) at the standard rates. Complex capital allowance claims (industrial buildings, specified intangible assets) may be charged separately.

- **Year-end review.** A meeting, phone call, or video call (typically 30 to 60 minutes) to review your tax position, explain your liability, discuss payment dates, and raise any planning points for the following year.

- **Basic advice.** Guidance on allowable business expenses, record-keeping requirements, and Revenue compliance. This covers routine questions asked during the year-end process.

### Limited Company Standard Package (EUR 1,500-3,500/year)

A typical limited company package includes everything in the sole trader package, plus:

- **Statutory financial statements.** Preparation of the annual accounts in accordance with FRS 102 or FRS 102 Section 1A, including profit and loss account, balance sheet, notes to the accounts, and directors' report.

- **Corporation tax return (CT1).** Filing the annual corporation tax return with Revenue, including trading income calculations, capital allowances, close company surcharge, and preliminary tax.

- **CRO annual return (B1).** Preparing and filing the annual return with abridged financial statements to the Companies Registration Office. The EUR 20 CRO filing fee is usually passed through to you separately.

- **Director's personal Form 11.** At least one director's personal income tax return, covering salary, dividends, benefit-in-kind, and any other personal income.

- **Company secretarial basics.** Monitoring the CRO annual return date and ensuring timely filing. Some firms include basic secretarial services (maintaining statutory registers) while others charge separately.

## Premium Package: What Extra Services Cost

Beyond the standard compliance package, accountants offer a range of additional services. Understanding what each costs helps you decide which services are worth paying for and which you can handle yourself.

### VAT Returns

VAT return preparation and filing is the most common add-on service.

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Single VAT3 return (from bookkeeping data) | EUR 50-100 |
| Single VAT3 return (from source documents) | EUR 100-150 |
| Annual RTD (Return of Trading Details) | EUR 50-100 |
| Full year (6 x VAT3 + RTD) | EUR 350-700 |

Some accountants include two or three VAT returns in their annual package. Others treat all VAT work as a separate engagement. When comparing quotes, always ask whether VAT returns are included.

### Payroll

Payroll processing is typically charged per employee per pay run:

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Monthly payroll per employee | EUR 5-10 per run |
| Weekly payroll per employee | EUR 5-15 per run |
| New employee setup | EUR 30-75 |
| Year-end reconciliation | EUR 100-200 |

For a company with five employees on monthly payroll, annual payroll costs through your accountant are approximately EUR 300 to EUR 600. This is in addition to your annual accounting fee.

### Management Accounts

Internal financial reporting for business decision-making:

| Frequency | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Quarterly management accounts | EUR 200-400 per quarter |
| Monthly management accounts | EUR 150-300 per month |

Management accounts typically include a profit and loss statement, balance sheet, and cash flow summary. Some firms add budget variance analysis, key performance indicators, and commentary on the results.

### Tax Planning and Advisory

Proactive tax planning beyond the scope of basic compliance:

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Annual tax planning review | EUR 200-500 |
| Incorporation analysis (sole trader to company) | EUR 200-400 |
| Director remuneration optimisation | EUR 150-300 |
| Retirement/succession planning | EUR 300-600 |
| Revenue audit representation (hourly) | EUR 150-300/hour |

These advisory services are where accountants add value beyond compliance. A good tax planning review can identify savings that far exceed the fee. The key is whether this service is included in your package or billed as an extra.

### Bookkeeping

If you want your accountant to handle ongoing bookkeeping:

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Monthly bookkeeping (under 100 transactions) | EUR 200-400/month |
| Monthly bookkeeping (100-300 transactions) | EUR 400-700/month |
| Year-end bookkeeping clean-up | EUR 300-800 |

Most accountants encourage clients to maintain their own bookkeeping in cloud software and hand over clean data. If the accountant must perform bookkeeping from source documents, this work is charged separately from the compliance package.

### Company Secretarial

Ongoing company secretarial services beyond the basic CRO annual return:

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Annual company secretarial package | EUR 200-500 |
| Individual CRO filing (director change, address change) | EUR 75-200 |
| Share transfer / allotment | EUR 100-300 |
| AGM minutes preparation | EUR 75-150 |

## Hidden Charges to Watch For

The most common source of frustration with accountant fees is unexpected charges for services the client assumed were included. Here are the charges most frequently caught out on:

### Phone Call and Email Charges

Some firms bill for every interaction beyond the annual review meeting. A five-minute phone call to ask about a VAT threshold or a quick email about an expense receipt could trigger a EUR 30 to EUR 75 charge. Other firms include reasonable ad-hoc queries in the annual fee.

**What to ask:** "How are phone calls and emails handled? Is there a limit on the number of queries included in the annual fee, or is every interaction billed separately?"

### Revenue Correspondence

When Revenue writes to you (seeking information, issuing assessments, or notifying you of a compliance check), your accountant handles the response. Some firms include this in the annual fee; others treat each piece of Revenue correspondence as a separate chargeable event at EUR 50 to EUR 200 per item.

**What to ask:** "Is handling routine Revenue correspondence included in the annual fee, or is it charged separately?"

### Late Submission Surcharges

If you provide your records to your accountant late, they may charge a premium for the additional time pressure. Common surcharges:

- **Records received less than 6 weeks before deadline:** 10-20% surcharge
- **Records received less than 2 weeks before deadline:** 25-50% surcharge
- **Rush processing (same week as deadline):** 50-100% surcharge

These surcharges are justified -- last-minute work disrupts the firm's scheduling and increases error risk -- but they should be communicated upfront in the engagement letter.

### Year-on-Year Increases

Some firms increase fees annually by 5 to 10 per cent without explicit justification. Over five years, a EUR 2,000 fee can grow to EUR 2,500 to EUR 3,000 without any change in the scope of work.

**What to ask:** "Is the quoted fee fixed for this year only, or for a defined period? What triggers a fee increase?"

### Photocopying and Administration

A small number of firms still charge for photocopying, postage, courier services, and administrative disbursements. These charges are typically modest (EUR 20 to EUR 50 per year) but feel petty and erode trust.

**What to ask:** "Are there any additional charges beyond the quoted fee for administrative items like postage or photocopying?"

## Fee Agreements and Engagement Letters

A professional accountant should provide a written engagement letter at the start of each year specifying:

1. **Scope of services.** Exactly what work is included in the quoted fee
2. **Fee amount.** The total annual fee and whether it is fixed or estimated
3. **Payment terms.** When payment is due (upfront, monthly, on completion) and accepted payment methods
4. **Additional charges.** How out-of-scope work is handled and at what rate
5. **Client responsibilities.** What you must provide and by when (records, information, signatures)
6. **Termination provisions.** How either party can end the engagement and what happens to your records

If your accountant does not provide an engagement letter, ask for one. The absence of a written agreement is a red flag -- it leaves scope, fees, and responsibilities undefined and makes disputes difficult to resolve.

## Fixed Fee vs Hourly Billing

### Fixed Fee (Most Common for Annual Compliance)

A fixed fee means you pay an agreed amount for a defined scope of work. This is the standard model for annual accounting packages in Ireland.

**Advantages:**
- Predictability -- you know exactly what you will pay
- Budgeting certainty
- Encourages efficiency -- the accountant is motivated to work efficiently because additional time does not generate additional revenue

**Disadvantages:**
- Scope is defined narrowly to protect the accountant's margin
- Any out-of-scope work triggers additional charges
- The accountant may under-invest time in advisory work if it is included in the fixed fee

### Hourly Billing (Common for Advisory and Ad-Hoc Work)

Hourly billing is used for work that cannot be easily scoped in advance: tax planning, Revenue negotiations, business restructuring, or one-off projects.

Typical hourly rates in Ireland:

| Level | Hourly Rate |
|---|---|
| Accounting technician | EUR 40-80 |
| Qualified accountant | EUR 100-200 |
| Tax specialist | EUR 150-300 |
| Senior partner | EUR 200-400 |

**Advantages:**
- You pay for actual work done
- No scope limitation -- the accountant works until the job is done
- Suitable for unpredictable or complex work

**Disadvantages:**
- Unpredictable total cost
- Potential for scope creep (work expanding beyond initial expectations)
- Time pressure anxiety (reluctance to ask questions because each query is billable)

### Negotiation Tips

**Ask for a fee cap on hourly work.** "I understand this will be billed hourly, but can we agree a maximum fee of EUR X? If the work is likely to exceed that, please contact me before proceeding."

**Request a blended rate.** If multiple team members will work on your account, ask for a single blended hourly rate rather than different rates for each person.

**Negotiate the fixed fee scope.** Rather than accepting the standard package, ask what it would cost to include two additional services that you currently pay separately for (e.g., VAT returns and basic tax planning).

## How to Compare Accounting Packages

When evaluating accountant quotes, follow this structured approach:

### Step 1: List Your Requirements

Before approaching accountants, document exactly what services you need:

- Annual accounts and tax return (Form 11 or CT1)
- CRO annual return (limited companies)
- VAT returns (how many per year)
- Payroll (how many employees, pay frequency)
- Bookkeeping (or do you handle this yourself)
- Director's personal Form 11
- Management accounts (frequency)
- Tax planning advice
- Ad-hoc queries (estimated frequency)

### Step 2: Request Itemised Quotes

Ask each accountant to quote against your specific requirements list. A proper quote should show:

1. What is included in the annual fee
2. What is charged separately, with unit prices
3. How ad-hoc queries are handled
4. Whether the fee is fixed or subject to review
5. Payment terms and schedule

### Step 3: Normalise for Comparison

Create a simple comparison table showing the total annual cost for identical services across each accountant. Include both the package fee and the estimated cost of add-on services you need.

| Service | Accountant A | Accountant B | Accountant C |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual accounts + CT1 | EUR 2,000 | EUR 1,800 | EUR 2,200 |
| VAT returns (6/year) | EUR 600 | Included | EUR 480 |
| Director's Form 11 | Included | Included | EUR 300 |
| Payroll (5 employees) | EUR 400 | EUR 500 | EUR 360 |
| Ad-hoc queries | 5 included | Hourly | 10 included |
| **Total estimated annual** | **EUR 3,000** | **EUR 2,300+** | **EUR 3,340** |

This comparison reveals that the apparently cheapest headline quote (Accountant B at EUR 1,800) may not be the cheapest in total because the hourly billing for ad-hoc queries is unpredictable.

### Step 4: Evaluate Beyond Price

Consider qualifications (ACA, ACCA, CPA), industry experience, responsiveness, technology use, and proactive communication. The cheapest accountant is not always the best value -- a proactive accountant who identifies EUR 2,000 in tax savings is better value than a cheaper one who simply files your return.

## Value vs Price

The best accounting relationship is one where your accountant's advice saves or earns you more than their fee. Signs that your accountant provides good value:

- They proactively suggest tax planning opportunities
- They alert you to legislative changes affecting your business
- They respond to queries within 24 to 48 hours
- They challenge your assumptions and offer alternative approaches
- They explain your accounts in plain language
- They help you understand your business's financial performance, not just your tax liability

Signs that you may be overpaying:

- You only hear from your accountant when they need your records
- Questions are met with additional invoices rather than helpful responses
- Your accounts are filed at the last possible moment
- There is no engagement letter or clear scope definition
- Fees increase annually without explanation
- You do not understand your own financial statements

The right accountant is an investment in your business, not just a compliance cost. When evaluating fees, consider the total value delivered -- compliance, planning, advice, and peace of mind -- not just the invoice amount.

Compare the proposed fee to the previous year and question any increase that is not justified by a genuine change in your business (more transactions, new employees, additional services).
