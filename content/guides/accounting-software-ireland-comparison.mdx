Choosing the right accounting software for your Irish small business can save you hours of bookkeeping each week, reduce errors, and give you real-time visibility into your financial position. The Irish market has several strong options, from international platforms like Xero and QuickBooks to Irish-developed solutions like Surf Accounts and Big Red Cloud that are built specifically for Irish tax and compliance requirements.

The best software for your business depends on your size, industry, budget, and whether you work with an accountant. This guide compares the leading options available to Irish businesses, explains the features that matter most, and helps you make an informed decision.

## What to Look for in Accounting Software

Before comparing individual products, understand the features that are essential for Irish businesses specifically:

### Irish VAT Support

Your accounting software must handle Irish VAT rates correctly. Ireland has four main VAT rates (23%, 13.5%, 9%, and 0%) plus exempt supplies. The software should allow you to assign the correct rate to each product or service and automatically calculate VAT on invoices. It should also support VAT return preparation, ideally generating a VAT3 return or at least providing the figures you need to complete one.

### Bank Feeds for Irish Banks

Automatic bank feeds import your transactions directly from your bank account into the software, dramatically reducing manual data entry. Check that the software supports feeds from your Irish bank. AIB, Bank of Ireland, and Permanent TSB are the most common banks used by Irish businesses. Revolut and N26 business accounts are increasingly popular and are also supported by most modern platforms.

### Revenue Filing Support

Some accounting software can submit returns directly to Revenue through the Revenue Online Service (ROS) API. Others generate the figures you need to file manually through ROS. Direct filing integration saves time, but manual filing through ROS is straightforward and most Irish accountants are comfortable with either approach.

### Multi-Currency

If you trade with customers or suppliers in the UK (sterling), EU (euro is standard but some countries have their own currencies), or further afield, multi-currency support is essential. The software should handle exchange rate conversions, track foreign currency gains and losses, and produce reports in your base currency.

### Accountant Access

Most Irish businesses work with an external accountant for year-end accounts and tax returns. Choose software that allows your accountant to log in directly. All of the major platforms offer accountant access, and most Irish accounting firms work with at least Xero and Sage. Ask your accountant which platforms they support before committing.

## Xero

Xero is one of the most popular cloud accounting platforms worldwide and has a strong presence in Ireland. Originally from New Zealand, Xero has invested significantly in its Irish market support.

### Pricing

Xero offers three plans for Irish businesses:

- **Starter:** Limited to 20 invoices and 5 bills per month. Suitable for very small businesses or freelancers with few transactions.
- **Standard:** Unlimited invoices and bills, bank reconciliation, and short-term cash flow forecasting. The most popular choice for Irish SMEs.
- **Premium:** Everything in Standard plus multi-currency and expense management. Recommended for businesses with international trade.

Prices start from approximately EUR 15 per month for Starter and rise to approximately EUR 40 per month for Premium (pricing may vary; check xero.com/ie for current rates).

### Irish-Specific Features

- Supports all Irish VAT rates and generates VAT return figures
- Bank feeds available for AIB, Bank of Ireland, and most Irish banks
- Irish payroll available through integrated partners
- Strong ecosystem of Irish accountants and bookkeepers who use Xero as their primary platform

### Strengths

- Clean, intuitive interface that non-accountants find easy to use
- Excellent mobile app for invoicing and expense capture on the go
- Largest app marketplace of any accounting platform (over 1,000 integrations)
- Automatic bank reconciliation with smart matching suggestions
- Real-time collaboration between you and your accountant

### Limitations

- Starter plan is too restrictive for most businesses
- No built-in payroll for Ireland (requires a partner integration)
- Can become expensive with add-ons for payroll, expenses, and projects

## Sage Business Cloud Accounting

Sage has a long history in the Irish market, having been one of the first accounting software providers to establish a presence in Ireland. Sage Business Cloud Accounting is their cloud-based offering for small businesses.

### Pricing

Sage offers two main tiers:

- **Accounting Start:** Invoicing, bank feeds, cash flow insights
- **Accounting:** Full accounting, VAT returns, quotes, purchase invoices

Prices typically range from EUR 12 to EUR 30 per month. Sage occasionally offers promotional pricing for new customers.

### Irish-Specific Features

- Built-in support for Irish VAT rates and VAT return preparation
- Direct bank feeds for Irish banks
- Irish payroll module available (Sage Payroll is widely used in Ireland)
- Irish customer support team

### Strengths

- Sage Payroll integration is one of the most popular payroll solutions in Ireland
- Strong reputation with Irish accountants, particularly those who have used Sage desktop products for years
- Good reporting and financial analysis tools
- Established brand with decades of Irish market presence

### Limitations

- Interface can feel dated compared to Xero
- Fewer third-party integrations than Xero
- Migration from Sage desktop to Sage cloud can be complex

## QuickBooks Online

QuickBooks Online, from Intuit, is the dominant accounting software in the US and has been growing its presence in Ireland and the UK market.

### Pricing

QuickBooks offers four plans:

- **Self-Employed:** Basic income and expense tracking for freelancers
- **Simple Start:** Invoicing, expense tracking, basic reports
- **Essentials:** Multiple users, bill management, time tracking
- **Plus:** Project tracking, inventory, budgeting

Prices range from approximately EUR 10 to EUR 35 per month. QuickBooks frequently offers significant discounts for the first few months.

### Irish-Specific Features

- Supports Irish VAT rates and generates VAT return figures
- Bank feeds available for major Irish banks
- Irish payroll through integrated partners
- Making Tax Digital (MTD) ready (relevant if you also have UK tax obligations)

### Strengths

- Very user-friendly interface, particularly for those new to accounting software
- Strong mobile app
- Good inventory tracking on higher-tier plans
- Affordable entry point, especially with promotional pricing

### Limitations

- Fewer Irish accountants use QuickBooks compared to Xero or Sage
- Some Irish-specific features are less mature than Xero or Sage
- The Self-Employed plan is quite basic

## Surf Accounts

Surf Accounts is an Irish-developed cloud accounting solution, built from the ground up for the Irish and UK markets. Being Irish-developed, it has native support for Irish tax and compliance requirements.

### Pricing

Surf Accounts offers competitive pricing for Irish businesses, with plans starting from approximately EUR 20 per month. Contact Surf Accounts directly for current pricing as they often provide custom quotes based on business size and requirements.

### Irish-Specific Features

- Built by an Irish team with deep understanding of Irish accounting requirements
- Full Irish VAT support including VAT3 return preparation
- Irish payroll module
- Designed around Irish accounting practices and terminology
- Direct support from an Irish-based team

### Strengths

- Purpose-built for the Irish market (no adaptation from US or Australian defaults)
- Strong feature set for the price
- Includes CRM and project management features
- Good for businesses that want everything in one platform
- Irish customer support during Irish business hours

### Limitations

- Smaller app ecosystem compared to Xero or QuickBooks
- Less well-known internationally, which may matter if you expand overseas
- Smaller community of accountants using the platform

## Big Red Cloud

Big Red Cloud is another Irish-developed accounting solution, headquartered in Dublin. It has been serving Irish businesses for over 30 years, making it one of the longest-established accounting software providers in the Irish market.

### Pricing

Big Red Cloud offers a straightforward pricing model starting from approximately EUR 20 per month. They also offer a desktop version for businesses that prefer locally installed software.

### Irish-Specific Features

- Developed specifically for the Irish market
- Full Irish VAT support
- Irish payroll included
- Revenue-ready reports
- Irish-based customer support and training

### Strengths

- Deep Irish market expertise built over three decades
- Includes payroll as standard (not an expensive add-on)
- Simple, no-nonsense interface designed for Irish SMEs
- Available as both cloud and desktop software
- Strong telephone support from their Dublin office

### Limitations

- Interface is functional rather than modern
- Limited third-party integrations
- Smaller user community than international platforms
- Marketing and documentation are less polished than larger competitors

## Free and Low-Cost Options

If you are just starting out or have a very small business with minimal transactions, free options exist:

### Spreadsheets

A well-structured spreadsheet (Excel or Google Sheets) can handle basic bookkeeping for a very small sole trader business. Revenue does not require you to use accounting software, only to maintain accurate records. However, spreadsheets become unwieldy as your business grows, offer no bank reconciliation, and are prone to errors.

### Wave

Wave is a free accounting platform that covers invoicing, expense tracking, and basic reporting. While it is a legitimate product, it is primarily designed for the North American market. Irish VAT support is limited, and you may need to work around the platform's defaults.

### Revenue's Own Tools

Revenue provides free tools for certain filings. ROS (Revenue Online Service) handles your tax returns directly. For PAYE purposes, Revenue's payroll notification system is free. These are not full accounting solutions but can supplement a basic bookkeeping setup.

## Choosing Based on Business Size

### Freelancers and Micro-Businesses (1-2 people, under EUR 50,000 turnover)

Xero Starter or QuickBooks Self-Employed/Simple Start. Your needs are basic: invoicing, expense tracking, and bank reconciliation. Choose whichever your accountant prefers.

### Small Businesses (3-10 employees, EUR 50,000-500,000 turnover)

Xero Standard, Sage Accounting, or Surf Accounts. You need proper double-entry bookkeeping, VAT return support, multi-user access, and ideally payroll integration. This is where Irish-developed options like Surf Accounts and Big Red Cloud become particularly competitive.

### Growing Businesses (10-50 employees, EUR 500,000+ turnover)

Xero Premium, QuickBooks Plus, or Sage with additional modules. You need multi-currency, project tracking, inventory management, and robust reporting. At this stage, your accountant's preference and integration requirements should heavily influence your choice.

## Working with Your Accountant

The single most important factor in choosing accounting software is compatibility with your accountant. Before committing to any platform:

1. Ask your accountant which platforms they actively support
2. Confirm they can log in to your account directly
3. Check whether they charge differently based on the platform you use (some accountants charge less if you use their preferred software because it reduces their work)
4. Discuss whether they will do your bookkeeping or whether you will handle it yourself

Most Irish accountants work with Xero and Sage. A growing number also support QuickBooks. Fewer work with Surf Accounts and Big Red Cloud, though these platforms are perfectly capable and your accountant can adapt.

If your accountant strongly recommends a specific platform, there is usually a good reason. They have experience with it, know its strengths and limitations, and can support you more efficiently. Unless you have a compelling reason to choose differently, follow their recommendation.

## Making the Switch

If you are currently using spreadsheets or legacy desktop software, moving to cloud accounting is one of the best operational improvements you can make. Most platforms offer a migration path:

- Import your chart of accounts, customer list, and supplier list
- Set up bank feeds
- Enter opening balances as at your migration date
- Start entering transactions from the migration date forward

Your accountant can help with the migration, and most software providers offer onboarding support. Plan the switch at the start of a VAT period or financial year to keep things clean.
